Dame Penelope Keith, acclaimed actress known for The Good Life and To the Manor Born, dies aged 86 after cancer

Unbiased summary

Dame Penelope Keith, the British actress best known for playing Margo Leadbetter in The Good Life (1975–1978) and Audrey fforbes-Hamilton in To the Manor Born (1979), died peacefully at her Surrey home after living with cancer. She was 86. Her family issued a statement expressing sadness and gratitude for the care she received, requesting privacy. Born Penelope Anne Constance Hatfield on 2 April 1940 in Sutton, Surrey, she trained as an actress and joined the Royal Shakespeare Company in 1963. She won BAFTA awards in 1977 and 1978, and an Olivier Award in 1976. She was appointed OBE, later CBE, and made a Dame in 2013. Felicity Kendal is now the sole surviving lead cast member of The Good Life.
