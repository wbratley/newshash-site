Twin earthquakes measuring 7.2 and 7.5 strike Venezuela, causing building collapses, state of emergency declared

Unbiased summary

Two powerful earthquakes struck near Venezuela's Caribbean coast on Wednesday 24 June, approximately 100 miles west of Caracas. The US Geological Survey recorded the first at magnitude 7.2 and the second, 39 seconds later, at magnitude 7.5. Buildings collapsed in Caracas and other areas, Simon Bolivar Airport was closed, and a tsunami advisory was briefly issued. Interim President Delcy Rodríguez declared a state of emergency. Early confirmed deaths were limited to a handful in Caracas districts, with an official toll of at least 32 dead and 700 injured later reported. The USGS issued a probabilistic estimate that deaths could range from 10,000 to 100,000, representing a statistical modelling range rather than a confirmed count. International aid offers followed, including from the United States and El Salvador.
