Man charged after five injured in suspected anti-Muslim knife attacks across Edinburgh, counter-terrorism police investigating

Unbiased summary

On Friday 19 June 2026, a series of violent incidents occurred across Edinburgh. Five men aged 22–39 were injured, four requiring hospital treatment. The attacks began near Broomhouse Mosque in west Edinburgh, where two men who had attended Asr prayers were approached in Sighthill Park. Further incidents occurred on Leith Walk, where CCTV footage showed a topless man driving erratically, abandoning his car, and attacking individuals with two large knives. A pizzeria door was also battered. Police Scotland arrested a 38-year-old white Scottish man and charged him in connection with multiple incidents including alleged threats, robbery and vandalism. Counter-terrorism police joined the investigation, though no terrorist designation had been made. None of the injuries were life-threatening. First Minister John Swinney condemned the attacks, and approximately 100 people held a community solidarity rally on Leith Walk on Sunday.
