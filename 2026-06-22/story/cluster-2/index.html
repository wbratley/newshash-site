Wowcher apologises for marketing email appearing to reference crocodile attack on three-year-old at Huntingdonshire zoo

Unbiased summary

Discount e-commerce site Wowcher issued an unreserved apology after a Saturday marketing email carried the subject line 'Snap up these deals quicker than a croc can catch a kid!' — wording that appeared to reference a real incident in which a three-year-old boy ended up in a crocodile enclosure at Johnsons of Old Hurst zoo near Huntingdon on Thursday. The boy suffered serious injuries and remains in a critical but stable condition at Addenbrooke's Hospital in Cambridge. A 30-year-old man, not known to the child, was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder but was bailed after police determined he was unfit for interview; he is bailed until 18 September. Cambridgeshire Police's Major Crime Unit is reviewing CCTV footage. Screenshots of the Wowcher email circulated on social media prompting widespread public criticism. Wowcher said the email was never approved and pledged to review its sign-off processes.
