Train driver Shaun Burton, 60, named among casualties of Bedford train collision that injured 100 people

Unbiased summary

On Friday 19 June 2025, two East Midlands Railway trains collided near Bedford on the line between Bedford and Luton. The 16:40 service from Corby struck the stationary 15:50 service from Nottingham, both bound for London St Pancras. Train driver Shaun Burton, 60, was killed at the scene. Of the 100 people injured, 11 suffered very serious injuries, 32 serious injuries, and 57 minor injuries; nine remained in a critical condition as of Saturday. The Rail Accident Investigation Branch and British Transport Police are investigating the cause. Network Rail described it as a 'tragic, isolated incident' and confirmed disruption to services until at least 28 June. Burton's family, his union Aslef, and East Midlands Railway all paid tribute to him.
