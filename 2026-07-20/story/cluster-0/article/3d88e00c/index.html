Spain 1-0 Argentina PLAYER RATINGS: Which two stars received 3/10 after dreadful displays? Who was 'imperious' but faded? And which Premier League stalwart delivered a 'shocking' first-half? Ferran Torres wrote his name into Spanish football history after scoring in the 106th minute of the World Cup final to break the hearts of Argentina and Lionel Messi. The substitute, who smashed his finish into the roof of the net, reclaimed deserved glory for La Roja for the first time since 2010 after their opponents managed just two shots across the entirety of the match. Spain's elegance and discipline was contrasted with the brutish skulduggery of Argentina, who saw Enzo Fernandez sent off in the 93rd minute. His midfield counterpart, Leandro Paredes, was also shown his marching orders after a violent clash after full-time. Daily Mail Sport's CRAIG HOPE was at the MetLife Stadium to bring you our player ratings. Spain have won the World Cup after a deserved 1-0 victory over Argentina on Sunday night It was a devastating end for Lionel Messi though who has likely played his last World Cup game Spain (4-2-3-1) Unai Simon - 6 Twice off his line in a flash to clear after balls were dropped in behind for Messi and Alvarez to chase. But there was not a save to make. Pedro Porro - 6 Hardly troubled in defence so took that opportunity to join attack, as he likes to. Heavily involved, - more than 120 touches - but crosses rarely found intended target. Pau Cubarsi - 8 Tried to get his team going with forward passes from deep, including one to get Yamal away. Was super cool in defence, too. He’s a star. Aymeric Laporte - 7 Rarely flustered at the back so subdued was Messi and thought he’d won it with late header plucked on goal-line by Martinez. Marc Cucurella - 7 Typically snappy in his challenges and, like Porro, poured forward when realising how passive Argentina were. Nearly scored opener with low drill wide. Rodri - 7 Imperious early on and everything went through him. He looked like the grown-up in midfield battle. That influence faded and he could not find the pass to break stubborn Argentina. Rodri dictated the game for Spain in midfield and ended up winning Player of the Tournament Pau Cubarsi was brilliant in defence and helped start Spain's attacks with his superb passing Fabian Ruiz - 6 A threat amid otherwise mundane first half, but way below best. Came into it more after break and dribble led to good chance for Torres. Much like whole tournament, not the final we’d hoped for from him. Lamine Yamal - 7 A threat amid otherwise mundane first half, but still below best. Came into it more after break and dribble led to good chance for Torres. Much like whole tournament, not really the final we’d hoped for from him, but played his part. Dani Olmo - 6 Like many of Spain’s other attacking starters he just could not produce that moment of inspiration. Not as peripheral as the others, but no real impact. Alex Baena - 4 No end product from left wing and was a surprise he stayed on until 75th minute. A big chance to impress but Atletico Madrid man did not take it. Mikel Oyarzabal - 5 A star of the tournament but this was a game to forget on the biggest occasion. One shot on target and one touch in area before making way just after hour. Kept his team in the game and had no chance with winner. Easily Argentina’s best. Gonzalo Montiel - 6 Saved likely goal when stealing from toe of Cucurella in penalty area after half-time, but that was his last involvement. Had been solid before that. Emerges with credit. Rodrigo De Paul - 5 His loose pass caused team-mate Lisandro to foul Rodri at expense of booking and injury. Was diligent in defensive work but not good enough on the ball. Alexis Mac Allister - 4 Shocking first half. Repeatedly found red with stray passes and was lucky to escape yellow for lunge on Olmo. Recovered to put in decent shift but quality still lacking. Enzo Fernandez - 3 Red card shame on back of untidy display. He set the tone against England but was too passive here, except when committing the two second-half fouls that brought deserved yellows. Nico Gonzalez - 3 Anonymous and invariably lost the ball when he did get it. Was no surprise to see him hooked at the break after completing only five passes. Lionel Messi - 5 One touch first 15 minutes and that was from kick off. Also pulled out of 50-50 with Simon. Justin Bieber got closer to the penalty area in normal time. Belatedly came to life in final moments and was dangerous. All too late, though.
