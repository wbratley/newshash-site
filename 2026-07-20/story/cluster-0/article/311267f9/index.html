World Cup final LIVE: Lionel Messi in tears as Spain crowned champions vs Argentina Spain have been crowned the champions of the world two years after securing the European crown having dominated Argentina in the final in New Jersey, with Ferran Torres' goal enough Spain have won the World Cup with a slender victory against Argentina in New Jersey. Ferran Torres gave the European champions the lead just minutes into the second period of extra time. His goal came after nearly two hours of goalless football at MetLife Stadium. Argentina were reduced to ten men before the extra 30 minutes, with Chelsea's Enzo Fernandez sent off for two bookable offence. There was also some drama before the game. Spain's pre-match training session was forced to be cancelled due to storms, while fans and members of the media have stumbled into serious issues getting into the stadium. Meanwhile, the pre-match closing ceremonies have ran over, with delays impacting the game itself. Make sure you stay tuned for live reaction to the final. Argentina starting XI: Martínez; Montiel, Romero, Lisandro, Tagliafico; De Paul, Fernández, Mac Allister, González; Messi, Álvarez. Spain starting XI: Unai Simón; Porro, Cubarsi, Laporte, Cucurella; Rodri, Fabián Ruiz; Alex Baena, Dani Olmo, Lamine Yamal; Oyarzabal. Key Events Spanish football fans took to the streets of London to celebrate their country’s 1-0 World Cup final victory over Argentina, PA reports. Videos circulating on social media showed revellers dancing on the roofs of cars in Piccadilly in the early hours of Monday. Fans could be seen waving flags and chanting loudly as car horns blared in the background. If you were supporting Argentina, you're probably not in the best of moods right now. But at least you haven't lost £1.1m on a bet, like Canadian rapper Drake has. The right-wing politician posted in Spanish on X a few moments ago. The message, translated into English, said: "WORLD CELEBRATION. "Given the concern about the celebrations for the achievement reached by the Argentine National Team, I inform the population that, depending on what the Players and the Technical Staff decide regarding the day to celebrate, it will be declared a national holiday." This might end up being the enduring image of this World Cup - the President photobombing the trophy lift. Celebrations are firmly underway across Spain. As Rodri lifted the trophy for his country, about 47 million people had already started the party... It's been a summer of football - but there is not long to wait if you want even more action. Some domestic competitions, like the Scottish league cup, have already started, as have the qualifying stages for the European club tournaments. In England, the league cup starts in just 18 days, and the EFL kicks off a week later - so it's a 25 day wait for league football to restart. The Premier League starts later than both - there are are only 32 days until when champions Arsenal host Coventry, who won the Championship last year. And, next summer, we have the women's World Cup to look forward to in Brazil, and the men's Euros in the UK and Ireland a year after that. It really never stops.
