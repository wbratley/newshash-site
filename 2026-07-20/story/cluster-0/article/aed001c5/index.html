Shortly after the full-time whistle, the US President made his way down to the field, where he was loudly booed by the crowd before making his way onto the stage alongside FIFA chief Gianni Infantino. Trump and Infantino handed out various awards to match officials and players, before officially welcoming Spain's players up to the stage to lift the World Cup trophy. The US President presented the prestigious title to Spain captain Rodri, who took great pleasure in raising it aloft in East Rutherford. Much like when he presented the Club World Cup title last summer, Trump stood awkwardly alongside the Spain squad as they lifted the trophy and celebrated wildly.
