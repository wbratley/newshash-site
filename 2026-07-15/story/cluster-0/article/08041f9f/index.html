World Cup LIVE: Spain blow France away and await England or Argentina as FIFA break rules All eyes are on England's World Cup semi-final clash with Argentina ahead of Lionel Messi's first showdown with the Three Lions Mikel Oyarzabal and Pedro Porro fired Spain into the World Cup final as France’s quest for glory fell apart in Dallas. Luis de la Fuente's side shut out a France team brimming with attacking talent to secure their place in Sunday's final in New Jersey. While England's World Cup semi-final clash with Argentina has been classified as a 'highest risk' match at the tournament following a meeting between the FBI, FIFA, officials and local police. There are concerns that issues between rival fans could flare up due to the historical divide between the two nations. Although there's a rivalry on the pitch, the bitterness between the two countrys transcends the sport, with the Falklands War in 1982, where 907 people lost their lives, at the heart of the rivalry. Only this week, the Argentinian foreign minister restaked a claim to the South American islands, which was quickly rebuffed by Prime Minister Kier Starmer. A police statement has been released after the meeting between officials and Atlanta Police Department said: "As Atlanta prepares to host an upcoming FIFA World Cup semi-final match and welcomes increased numbers of residents and visitors, the Atlanta Police Department has enhanced its citywide public safety and security posture. "Additional personnel and resources are already deployed and will continue to be strategically assigned in and around the event venues, entertainment districts, and other high-traffic areas to help ensure a safe and enjoyable experience for everyone. These proactive measures are designed to protect the public, deter criminal activity, and ensure residents and visitors can safely enjoy this historic event." England head into the game with Argentina looking to banish the demons from the Azteca, where Diego Maradona infamously knocked the Three Lions out of the 1986 World Cup thanks to a moment of brilliance and the 'hand of God'. Lionel Messi - who will face England for the first time in his international career - and co will sport their darker blue kit for the clash on Wednesday night. FIFA accepted their request to shift away from their traditional light blue and white stripes, with the press in Argentina focusing on the superstitious kit, deeming it good luck. The two sides also now have confirmation of who will take charge of the fixture, after referee Ismail Elfath was appointed for the semi-final clash in Atlanta. He said: "We’ve been dealing with this situation for three games now, there have been too many fouls. "We’re talking about 10 or 15 fouls where the kid [Lamine Yamal] goes to the ground. If the referees do not call them, then the defence will keep doing the same thing. "The permissiveness is clear and was especially obvious today. But he still played a great match." I’ll be back here tomorrow.” Kylian Mbappe appeared to question Didier Deschamps' approach and tactics following France’s World Cup defeat to Spain. Fabian [Ruiz] and Rodri had plenty of time to play. “There was a lack of communication on the press. I think we should have done man-to-man press and force them to run with us." Before he added: "We didn't play the game we wanted, technically, tactically. When you don't do what you have to do in a World Cup semifinal, you don't win. “Spain respected their game plan and what the team usually does. They like to control the ball and the tempo. Our plan was to press them high so they could not install their rhythm. "Because they are better than us at controlling a game. We didn't manage to do it. We were too sloppy technically. But if we are objective, we didn't put all the ingredients to go to the final.” Argentina boss Lionel Scaloni has tried to ease potential tensions ahead of his side’s World Cup semi-final with England.
