IOC lifts Russian Olympic Committee suspension, opening path to LA 2028 participation with conditions still pending

Unbiased summary

The International Olympic Committee's executive board has provisionally lifted its suspension of the Russian Olympic Committee, which had been in place since October 2023 following Russia's invasion of Ukraine. The IOC determined that the ROC no longer holds membership of regional sports organisations in Ukrainian-occupied territories. Russian athletes will now be eligible to compete, subject to strict anti-doping conditions overseen by the International Testing Agency rather than Russian authorities. The previous vetting process requiring athletes to not publicly support the war has been removed. No decision has been made on whether Russia can display its flag, anthem, or colours at LA 2028. The IOC will not host events in Russia or invite Russian government officials to IOC events. The IOC stated it continues to strongly condemn Russia's invasion of Ukraine. Individual sports federations retain authority over their own eligibility rules.

The IOC president's quote about athlete responsibility is included but positioned after critical framing, subtly questioning its sincerity.
