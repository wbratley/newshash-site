Argentina beat Egypt 3-2 in World Cup last-16 thriller after recovering from 2-0 deficit, Messi scores and assists

Unbiased summary

Argentina defeated Egypt 3-2 in their 2026 World Cup last-16 match in Atlanta, coming back from 2-0 down with approximately 13 minutes remaining. Egypt scored through Yasser Ibrahim and Mostafa Zico, while Argentina's goalkeeper Mostafa Shobeir had a strong performance. Lionel Messi, 39, missed a first-half penalty — his second miss of the tournament and fourth in World Cup history — but then provided an assist for Cristian Romero before equalising himself. Enzo Fernández headed the winner from a Lautaro Martínez cross, though Egypt disputed a foul in the buildup. Messi, who extended his World Cup record to 21 goals, was visibly emotional at full-time. Argentina advance to the quarter-finals. The comeback is reportedly the first time a team has overturned a two-goal deficit so late at a World Cup.
