Nigel Farage resigns as Clacton MP to trigger by-election amid two parliamentary standards investigations into undeclared donations

Unbiased summary

Nigel Farage announced his resignation as MP for Clacton on 8 July 2025, triggering a by-election he intends to contest. He faces two parliamentary standards investigations: one into a £5 million gift from crypto-billionaire Christopher Harborne received before the 2024 general election, and another into undeclared financial support — including security and staffing costs — provided by convicted fraudster George Cottrell. Farage denied wrongdoing, framed the by-election as 'people versus the establishment', and accused Sky News and The Times of harassing his family. Labour, the Conservatives, the Liberal Democrats, the Green Party, and Restore Britain all declined to field candidates. Professor Sir John Curtice assessed Farage as likely to win given his 46% vote share in 2024 and Reform's continued strong national polling, though noted the decision carries some risk.
