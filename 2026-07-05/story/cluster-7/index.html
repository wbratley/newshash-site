England prepare for Mexico World Cup last-16 tie with Quansah fit, James doubtful, amid security presence at team hotel

Unbiased summary

England are preparing to face co-hosts Mexico in a World Cup Round of 16 match. Manager Thomas Tuchel confirmed that defender Jarell Quansah, who missed the 2-1 win over DR Congo with an ankle injury, has returned to full training and is available. Reece James, absent for two matches with a hamstring strain sustained against Ghana, is doubtful but may make the bench pending a medical assessment. The England squad trained at Pumas' facility in Mexico City. Separately, hundreds of Mexican supporters gathered outside England's hotel, with over 100 riot police deployed to maintain order. Mexican authorities also confirmed 17,000 police officers would be on duty on match day. Former England manager Sam Allardyce publicly criticised Tuchel's squad selection, citing the omission of experienced players including Luke Shaw, Harry Maguire, and Trent Alexander-Arnold.
