Trump delivers speech at Washington DC event marking America's 250th anniversary, delayed by severe weather

Unbiased summary

On 4 July 2025, President Donald Trump delivered a speech on the National Mall in Washington DC as part of a 'Salute to America' celebration marking the United States' 250th anniversary. The event was delayed by severe weather including heavy rain, lightning and strong winds, prompting the evacuation of thousands of attendees. In his address, Trump praised American achievements, referenced England's defeat in the Revolutionary War, rejected communism, and vowed America would remain the dominant nation on Earth. Separately, Pope Leo — an American-born pontiff — sent a letter congratulating the US on its anniversary, in which he also emphasised the importance of welcoming immigrants. GB News broadcast from Philadelphia, where reporter Dougie Beattie observed crowds of patriotic Americans celebrating the occasion.
