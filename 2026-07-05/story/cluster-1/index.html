London Pride 2026: Over 35,000 march through central London as organisers confirm Madonna will not appear

Unbiased summary

Pride in London's annual LGBTQ+ parade took place on Saturday 4 July 2026, with more than 35,000 marchers from approximately 600 groups walking from Hyde Park Corner to Whitehall via Piccadilly. Mayor of London Sadiq Khan participated at the front of the march. Organisers expected over one million spectators in the capital. Performers Beth Ditto and MNEK headlined the Trafalgar Square stage. Organisers confirmed via social media that Madonna, who had been rumoured to appear, would not perform. Pride in London's theme was 'An Alliance of Defiance'. The organisation had also faced a legal dispute with its former chief executive. Organisers cited concerns including NHS gender-affirming care waiting lists exceeding four years in some regions, the absence of a comprehensive conversion therapy ban, closure of 58% of London's LGBTQ+ venues since 2006, and over 18,000 sexual orientation hate crimes reported in 2025.

Coverage by outlet
The Guardian left
Angle Frames Pride primarily as a political protest movement with urgent, unresolved LGBTQ+ rights issues requiring continued activism.
Bias The Guardian gives substantial space to activist voices and Pride's official warnings about trans healthcare waiting lists, conversion therapy legislation, venue closures, and hate crime statistics, lending the coverage a campaigning tone. It includes historical context about the 1972 march and Stonewall, reinforcing the protest framing. It largely omits the organisational controversy around the former chief executive and gives minimal attention to the celebratory or entertainment aspects of the event, downplaying these compared to other outlets.
