London Pride 2026 recap as headliners take to stage in Trafalgar Square

London Pride 2026 recap as headliners take to stage in Trafalgar Square London's hotly anticipated LGBTQ+ Pride parade drew hundreds of thousands to the capital, turning the city into a sea of colour London's Pride parade has turned the capital's streets into a sea of colour as hundreds of thousands gathered to support the LGBTQ+ community. More than 35,000 marchers from around 600 groups walked from Hyde Park Corner to Westminster in this year's Pride in London parade on Saturday. The iconic march passed several major London landmarks, including Trafalgar Square and Piccadilly Circus. The procession began at midday, with Mayor of London Sadiq Khan among those leading chants at the front. American singer-songwriter Beth Ditto and British performer MNEK were among the artists to appear in Trafalgar Square. Organisers confirmed on Saturday evening that Madonna would not be making an appearance or performing at the event amid online rumours. In a call for the LGBTQ+ community to stand together and support one another, this year's event was centred on the campaign "An Alliance of Defiance". The Mirror was an official media partner for the event. Key Events That concludes our live coverage of this year's Pride in London event. Happy Pride to everyone celebrating! Jeremy Corbyn, MP for Islington North, has wished everyone celebrating in London a happy Pride. "With hate crimes against the LGBTQ+ community rising in the UK, we must support the fight for rights, protections and dignity for all," he wrote on X. Zack Polanski has wished people a happy Pride on X, sharing a selfie with his partner and an image of crews from London Fire Brigade (LFB) at today's parade. The Green Party leader said it was a "huge honour" to march alongside firefighters, crew and support teams from the brigade. Hundreds of thousands of people marched through London in the capital’s LGBTQ+ Pride parade today as campaigners warned rights were "being taken away from trans people". Pride in London organisers said more than 35,000 marchers from about 600 groups made their way from Hyde Park Corner to Whitehall via Piccadilly. Mayor of London Sir Sadiq Khan was among those at the front of the march and led people in a chant of "Happy Pride". Companies sponsoring floats at the parade included Lidl, Tesco and Ikea, with the Swedish furniture brand’s float carrying the message: "Love doesn’t require instructions." London-based football teams including Arsenal, West Ham and Crystal Palace also had floats in the parade. Madonna will not be attending the Pride in London event today, organisers have said. "In response to the rumours, we can confirm that Madonna will not be appearing or performing at Pride in London," the organisation wrote in a post on social media. "We have two fantastic headliners at Trafalgar Square to conclude a brilliant day of protest and celebration." It is understood that there had been communication between organisers and Madonna but that she was never a confirmed attendee. Pride in London interim chief executive Rebecca Paisis said: "We want 2026 to be the most inclusive Pride in London event yet. Our movement has always been built on many voices becoming one united front - from the people who marched in 1972 to those joining us for the first time this year. That’s where our power lies. "As LGBTQ+ people, we’ve never been strangers to adversity, but neither are we strangers to collective action. This year’s campaign is a reminder that whilst the community often faces challenges in isolation, it is by coming together that we can change history." A spokesperson for Pride in London said: "The urgency is clear: NHS gender-affirming care waiting lists now exceed four years in some regions while a comprehensive trans-inclusive ban on conversion therapy remains uncodified into law despite a 2018 government pledge. "At the same time, the community infrastructure LGBTQ+ people rely on is shrinking - since 2006, 58% of London’s LGBTQ+ venues have closed. Together, these gaps in care, protection and safe spaces are unfolding amid continued hostility, with Home Office figures showing that more than 18,000 hate crimes motivated by sexual orientation were reported to police in 2025." Gay rights activist Julian Hows said the Pride in London march is especially important in the context of "rights being taken away from trans people" in the UK. Speaking at the front of the parade, Mr Hows, 70, who was expelled from school for early gay rights activism in 1971, told PA: "Pride is important every year. I’ve been coming to Pride marches since 1972 when the policemen outnumbered the marchers." Mr Hows, who wore a rainbow-coloured waistcoat with Abseil Against Section 28 and Gay Liberation Front pins, added: "Pride is also important because it needs to have an underlying level of protest, and you can see the freedoms that we have can so easily be taken away. "We also always need to push further because there’s always somewhere where our rights are being taken away, whether it’s in this country with Reform, whether it’s [in] this country with rights being taken away from trans people, whether it’s abroad in other countries." Speaking at the march, Peter Tatchell told PA: "This year’s Pride is as important as ever, particularly now that Reform councils across the country are banning Pride flags … wanting to remove books from shelves. That is very dangerous, very threatening to all of us." The Australian-born activist also said FIFA was "doing nothing" about 11 countries banning gay footballers at the World Cup. "We’re marching in London Pride today to highlight the fact that at the World Cup, which is ongoing right now, 11 countries ban gay footballers from their team - that’s against FIFA rules, but FIFA is doing nothing," he said. A video posted on social media shows MP for Brent East Dawn Butler, Mayor of London Sadiq Khan and deputy mayors Howard Dawber, Mete Coban and Tom Copley marching together in high spirits at Pride in London. In a post on X, Mayor of London Sadiq Khan said: "Pride is both a celebration and a protest. Today we celebrate the progress we've made, while recommitting to the fight for equality. "At a time when rights and representation cannot be taken for granted, London stands with its LGBTQIA+ communities." Prominent human rights and LGBTQ+ activist Peter Tatchell is in the London Pride parade today - with banners calling on FIFA to do more about teams playing in the World Cup which 'ban gay players'. In a post on X, he said FIFA must suspend the teams, adding: "11 countries criminalise & discriminate against gay footballers." "This is against FIFA rules that ban discrimination based on sexuality But FIFA does nothing!" In another post, he slammed the official Pride London placards that read 'An alliance of defiance' and 'Many voices, one front' as "meaingless". "No condemnation of the culture war against trans people or Labour relegating the ban on conversion practices to a mere DRAFT Bill," Tatchell penned. "No calling out the #Commonwealth over 29 countries than criminalise us SHAME!" Headline acts are now kicking off at Trafalgar Square with Leo Kalyan currently performing. The Mexican Embassy in the UK is supporting London Pride today. The embassy said in a post on X: "Mexico present at Pride In London! "The Embassy of Mexico in the United Kingdom participated in this celebration that recognizes diversity, inclusion, and equality, reaffirming our country's commitment to defending the human rights of LGBTIQ+ people. "Mexico marches with pride, facing the world and true to who we are: a diverse, plural nation committed to building more just and inclusive societies. We continue celebrating pride as an expression of freedom, dignity, and the right of all people to be who they are." The support comes as England prepare to face off against Mexico in the World Cup on Monday. Piccadilly Circus is looking lively already this afternoon with London Pride celebrations and the parade well underway. The huge parade is well and truly underway now with spots of sun also making an appearance for those making their way through the capital. Rumour has it Madonna could make a surprise appearance at Pride in London today - which will undoubtedly cause "absolute pandemonium". It is tipped the icon, a passionate and long-time LGBTQ+ ally and advocate, could make an appearance on the main stage, though nothing has been confirmed by organisers. An insider previously told The Sun: "Organisers are playing it by ear in case of any last-minute changes but as it stands, Madonna is primed to come out on the main stage. She isn’t going to be performing like she did in New York. It’s more her coming out, speaking to the crowd and then heading off." Read more about her possible impromptu performance here. Brooklyn Brewery, the official beer partner of Pride in London, is due to bring a touching tribute in homage of the legendary Stonewall Inn in New York City to this year's parade. The Stonewall Inn was one off few venues in the big Apple where LGBTQ+ could meet openly and dance. It ended up becoming one of the most important pieces in LGBTQ history as it centred an uprising on the streets for queer rights. The spirit of the bar is anticipated to head for Pride today - with a refurbished bus, reimaginaged as the original Greenwich Village establishment - set to join the route. As much as London Pride is a celebration, it's also a day of activism. The event shines a spotlight on some of the critical issues the LGBTQ+ community faces today with some signs by parade-goers and spectators tipping to some of these concerns. One parent can be seen holding a sign that reads: "I love my trans non-binary child. They're awesome!" Another appears to be protesting against the 2026 World Cup. Signs read: "World Cup: 11 counties ban gay footballers. SHAME!" "Hate has no home here", reads another wrapped around the signature rainbow flag with a heart symbol. Bikers kicked off the start of London's Pride parade this afternoon, and now we have the flagbearers transporting the giant rainbow flag - the symbol of LGBTQ pride, and of course, a party float with music. The festivities are now well underway as spectators and parade-goers can be heard blowing whistles, singing along and dancing. We're off now - the London Pride parade has started. A quick glimpse at the front of the parade shows dozens of bikers, of course donning rainbow flags, transgender flags and signs. Hundreds of thousands of parade goers are now marching through the capital, setting off from Hyde Park towards Piccadilly Circus. The parade will continue to head south down Haymarket and Trafalgar Square before the finale at Whitehall. Spectators heading out to the parade this afternoon are being encouraged to use the following stations: - Bond Street - Charing Cross - Embankment - Leicester Square - Oxford Circus - Tottenham Court Road - Victoria The Mayor of London Saqid Khan is showing his support to the LGBTQ+ community by attending the London Pride event again today. Khan has been spotted sporting an England FC t-shirt, in anticipation of England v Mexico's World Cup match in the early hours of Monday morning. In a post on X, the Mayor wrote: "Happy Pride, London! "At my Pride Reception earlier this week, we celebrated love, authenticity and the trailblazers who paved the way for our LGBTQIA+ communities to live openly and proudly today." In another post he penned: "Today at Pride in London, we’ll show the world that love will always triumph over hate." The big parade comes today as Pride In London confirmed earlier this year it would bid for WorldPride 2032 on the event's 60th anniversary. The giant WorldPride event takes place every two years and invites the international LGBTQ+ community and its allies to celebrate the community in a huge 10-day festival. Pride In London’s interim CEO and Co-Chair of WorldPride Bid 2023, Rebecca Paisis said: “Pride began as protest, and that spirit remains at the heart of everything we do. “Marking 60 years since London’s first march by hosting WorldPride 2032 would give us the opportunity to welcome the world to our city. “It would unite us in solidarity and action at a time when LGBTQIA+ rights are being challenged globally.” There is a dedicated family and youth zone over in Victoria Embankment Gardens. The line-up for performances there include Aida from Drag Queen Story Hour, Andi from Dadda and Daddy, Badge Cafe, Brass United Social Club, Face painting by Carnivelle Party, David Ribi, Emma from at your Beat!, Joelle D’Fontaine, Make with, Norvil & Josephine, and Pop’n’Olly. Soho Square is set to see some incredible speeches and performances from across the trans and non-binary community. A dedicated trans-led stage has been curated by Mzz Kimberley. Performers include Brandy x Temptation, Curvy Black Witch, Custodians, Dr Helen Webberley, Ebony Rose / Para Pride, Frigid Presents Mother, ISH99NI, Lexi & Aisha, M.E.N Live, Marty Davies, Nathan Ethan, Panty Soaker Sound System, Tabs from Butch, Please!, Taman, DJ Tasty Tim, Telisa TK, The New Cross Dresser, Trans Legal Clinic, Women of Colour in the Global Women Strike.
