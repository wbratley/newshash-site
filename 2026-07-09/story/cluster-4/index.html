Nigel Farage resigns as Clacton MP, triggering by-election he plans to contest amid financial gifts investigation

Unbiased summary

Nigel Farage resigned as MP for Clacton on 8 July 2025, triggering a by-election in which he intends to stand again. His resignation came while he faced a parliamentary standards investigation into an undeclared £5 million gift from cryptocurrency billionaire Christopher Harborne and questions about gifts from convicted fraudster George Cottrell. Farage framed the by-election as 'people versus the establishment.' Labour, the Conservatives, the Liberal Democrats, the Greens, and Restore Britain all declined to field candidates, branding the contest a political stunt. With major parties absent, satirical candidate Count Binface — comedian Jon Harvey — emerged as the primary rival. Betting odds on Binface shortened significantly. Lawrence Fox's Reclaim Party also announced a candidacy. Keir Starmer called it a 'desperate political stunt'; Kemi Badenoch called it a 'fake by-election.'
