Metropolitan Police launches investigation into £37,500 foreign donation allegation linked to Robert Jenrick's 2024 Conservative leadership campaign

Unbiased summary

The Metropolitan Police has opened a formal investigation following a January 2025 referral from the Electoral Commission into allegations that £37,500 of a donation to Robert Jenrick's 2024 Conservative leadership campaign originated from a foreign source, which would breach UK electoral law. The donation in question formed part of £100,000 given to Jenrick's campaign via British businessman Phillip Ullmann through a company called Spott Fitness. Investigators are examining claims that the £37,500 actually originated from Innovyz, a US company founded by Gary Klopfenstein, who pleaded guilty to wire fraud in 2024. Jenrick, who has since defected from the Conservatives to Reform UK, denies the allegations and says he has had no contact with police. The Met has not named any individuals under investigation.
