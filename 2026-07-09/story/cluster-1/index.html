Arthur Fery beats ninth seed Flavio Cobolli in straight sets to reach Wimbledon semi-finals as wildcard

Unbiased summary

British wildcard Arthur Fery, ranked 114th in the world before the tournament, defeated ninth seed Flavio Cobolli 6-4, 7-6(4), 6-0 in straight sets on Centre Court to reach the Wimbledon semi-finals. The 23-year-old, who received a wildcard entry having had only two prior grand slam wins, becomes the second wildcard in the Open Era to reach the Wimbledon men's singles semi-finals, after Goran Ivanisevic in 2001, and one of only five British men to reach the last four at Wimbledon. He will face French Open champion and second seed Alexander Zverev in the semi-finals. Queen Camilla, attending her fourth consecutive Wimbledon Day 10, greeted both Fery and Cobolli before the match and congratulated Fery afterwards. Princess Catherine also posted congratulations on X.
