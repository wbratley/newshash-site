David Beckham and Piers Morgan lead celebs celebrating World Cup quarter-final win

Article text

David Beckham and Piers Morgan lead celebs celebrating World Cup quarter-final win England secured their place in the World Cup semi-finals with a 2-1 quarter-final victory over Norway and a host of famous faces celebrated the Three Lions' triumph Sir David Beckham and Oasis frontman Liam Gallagher were amongst the star-studded crowd cheering on England's 2-1 World Cup quarter-final triumph over Norway. England will now face Argentina in the semi-finals after Jude Bellingham's brace secured the Three Lions' place in the last four. Sir David, who had visited the England squad during a training session in Miami earlier this week, took to his Instagram Stories to share a collection of photos and videos from the match, writing: "Brilliant from the lads." The Beckham children were also spotted alongside their parents in the stands throughout the match. In a further post, the former England international paid tribute to Jude Bellingham's stunning two-goal performance with a photo of the midfielder captioned "Hey Jude". Lady Victoria Beckham posted a picture of herself and her husband watching the game with three of their children, captioning it: "Special moment tonight in Miami with my family and for our country." Oasis singer Liam Gallagher echoed those sentiments, making clear he was desperate to see England lift the World Cup trophy. Throughout the tournament, the band's iconic track Wonderwall has emerged as an unofficial anthem for the national side, with players lining up after matches to belt it out alongside supporters. Following the victory, Gallagher posted on X: "We're winning this WC I don't know how don't care respect to all those players manager you gotta make it happen LG." In a personally signed message on X, the Prince of Wales also praised the squad for a "top performance in a tough environment". The future king posted: "Well done England! Top performance in a tough environment. This is a special team. Commiserations to a proud Norway team. Onto the semi final... Never in doubt! W". Olly Murs was amongst those caught up in the pre-match buzz, as the men's national side geared up to take on the Scandinavian nation at Miami Stadium in Florida. Murs posted a clip of himself belting out Wonderwall following the victory. Other celebrities who turned up to watch the match in the States included Mick Jagger, tennis icon Venus Williams, Love Island host Maya Jama and comedian Jack Whitehall, who reposted pictures from the venue after the triumph. Crown Prince Haakon of Norway was also seen in the crowd alongside Fifa president Gianni Infantino. Rock star Billy Idol wrote on X: "Fantastic seeing England reach the semifinal of the World Cup really close though Norway played really well we were lucky in some ways.. I was on the edge of my seat!". Meanwhile, Piers Morgan paid tribute to England boss Thomas Tuchel, describing him as a "natural-born winner who refuses to accept anything but the best". Prior to kick-off, Stranger Things star Millie Bobby Brown, 22, posted a snap of herself sporting an oversized red and white shirt emblazoned with the text " Fifa World Cup 2026", which she paired with a black beret, sunglasses and a pair of gold hoop earrings. Emmy-winning actor Mike Myers also threw his weight behind the England squad, posting a picture of himself saluting while wearing the iconic red tunic and black bearskin cap worn by the Guards regiments in the UK. Below the image, the actor and comedian, celebrated for blockbuster film franchises including Shrek and Austin Powers, penned: "Waiting for tonight's World Cup match! Go team England." Actor Ross Kemp, renowned for portraying hardman Grant Mitchell in BBC soap EastEnders, posted a sweaty clip from the gym before the fixture, discussing "positive vibes". The actor declared: "We, as a nation now, have to think incredibly positive. Take the doubts out of your mind, think positive, because Norway are going to be a very, very difficult team to beat. As a nation, we can do it. We know as a team, they can do it. So we really need to get behind them. Positive vibes, send the vibes over the Atlantic to Miami."
