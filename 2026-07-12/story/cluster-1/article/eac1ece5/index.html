Devon and Cornwall Police revealed she was killed at 12.30pm on Wednesday in the remote house where she lived alone with her cat on the edge of Dartmoor National Park. Widdecombe lay dead for nearly 24 hours before her body was discovered. A Devon and Cornwall Police spokesperson has now confirmed a 28-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of her murder. They said: "We can confirm that a 28-year-old man was arrested at an address in the South Yorkshire area this evening [11 July] on suspicion of the murder of Ann Widdecombe. "The suspect, who is a white British national, is now in police custody. Miss Widdecombe’s family have been informed of this development. "We were supported by Counter Terrorism Policing North East and South Yorkshire Police who carried out the arrest on behalf of Devon & Cornwall Police. At this time, there is still no information to suggest that this is a terrorism related incident and as a force we retain primacy of the investigation."
