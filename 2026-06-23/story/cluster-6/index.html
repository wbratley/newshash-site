Ten years after the Brexit vote: UK politics transformed, Starmer resigns, leave voters reflect on mixed outcomes

Unbiased summary

On 23 June 2026, the UK marked ten years since the Brexit referendum of 23 June 2016, in which 52% of voters chose to leave the European Union. The vote triggered significant political upheaval: David Cameron resigned immediately, and the UK has since had multiple prime ministers. Labour's Keir Starmer announced his resignation around the anniversary, citing a sluggish economy and political dysfunction. The UK formally left the EU on 31 January 2020, with trade arrangements taking effect from 1 January 2021. A decade on, assessments of Brexit's impact remain contested: some original leave voters report economic hardship and unmet promises, particularly in industries like fishing, while broader debate continues over sovereignty, immigration, trade costs, and long-term political consequences.
