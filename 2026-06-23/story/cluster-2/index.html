Jeffrey Donaldson convicted of 18 child sexual offences including rape; wife found to have aided offending

Unbiased summary

A jury at Newry Crown Court found former DUP leader Sir Jeffrey Donaldson, 63, guilty of 18 historical sexual offences against two women who were children at the time of the abuse, spanning 1985 to 2008. The offences included one count of rape, 13 counts of indecent assault, and four counts of gross indecency. Donaldson had pleaded not guilty and denied all charges throughout the four-week trial. His wife, Eleanor Donaldson, 60, was found to have aided and abetted his offending in five charges but was deemed unfit to stand trial on mental health grounds, so faced a trial of the facts rather than a criminal conviction. Judge Paul Ramsey remanded Donaldson into immediate custody and indicated a lengthy prison sentence was inevitable. Sentencing is scheduled for later in the year.
