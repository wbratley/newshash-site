Edinburgh man charged with terrorism-linked attempted murder after series of attacks injuring five people

Unbiased summary

Lewis Hawkes, 36, from Edinburgh, appeared at Edinburgh Sheriff Court on Monday charged with five counts of attempted murder, one count of assault and robbery, two counts of breach of the peace, and two counts of culpable and reckless conduct. All charges are aggravated by reason of a terrorist connection. The charges relate to a series of incidents across Edinburgh on Friday evening beginning around 20:30. Two 22-year-old men were stabbed multiple times near Broomhouse Mosque in Sighthill; three further men were attacked in the Telford Road and Leith Walk areas. Five people were injured in total, with three requiring hospital treatment; none sustained life-threatening injuries. Counter-terrorism officers are leading the investigation. The attacks are suspected to have an anti-Muslim motivation. Hawkes made no plea, was remanded in custody, and is due to reappear within eight days.
