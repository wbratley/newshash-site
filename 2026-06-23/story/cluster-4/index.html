World Cup 2026: Messi breaks all-time scoring record, France beat Iraq after storm delay, Algeria beat Jordan

Unbiased summary

At the 2026 FIFA World Cup, Lionel Messi scored twice for Argentina in a 2-0 win over Austria, taking his World Cup goal tally to 18 and surpassing Miroslav Klose's previous record of 17. Messi had earlier missed a penalty in the same match. Argentina qualified for the last 32 with six points from two games. In Philadelphia, France defeated Iraq 3-0 with Kylian Mbappe scoring twice and Ousmane Dembele adding a third, though the match was delayed over two hours at half-time due to severe thunderstorms. Mbappe was also observed directing ground staff clearing water from the pitch during the delay. In another Group J fixture, Algeria came from behind to beat Jordan 2-1, with Amine Gouiri scoring an 82nd-minute winner. England goalkeeper Jordan Pickford faced scrutiny following an uncharacteristically uncertain performance in England's win over Croatia.
