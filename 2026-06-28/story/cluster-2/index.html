Steve Clarke resigns as Scotland head coach after World Cup group-stage elimination, ending seven-year tenure

Unbiased summary

Steve Clarke resigned as Scotland men's head coach on Saturday after Scotland were eliminated from the 2026 FIFA World Cup at the group stage. Their exit was confirmed when Croatia defeated Ghana, rendering Scotland's results insufficient to progress as one of the best third-placed teams. Scotland finished Group C with three points, winning 1-0 against Haiti, losing 1-0 to Morocco, and losing 3-0 to Brazil. Clarke had signed a new four-year contract approximately one month before the tournament began. He had been in charge since May 2019, leading Scotland to two European Championships and their first World Cup since 1998. In an open letter to supporters, Clarke praised his players and wished his successor well. The Scottish FA confirmed his departure in an official statement.
