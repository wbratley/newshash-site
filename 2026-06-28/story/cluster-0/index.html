US and Iran exchange military strikes over Strait of Hormuz tanker attack, raising fears ceasefire is collapsing

Unbiased summary

A series of military exchanges between the US and Iran escalated over approximately 48 hours. Iran's IRGC attacked two commercial vessels near the Strait of Hormuz, including the Panama-flagged tanker M/T Kiku carrying over two million barrels of crude oil. The US responded with strikes on ten Iranian military targets, including surveillance infrastructure, communications systems, air defence sites, drone storage facilities, and minelayer capabilities, citing violations of an existing ceasefire memorandum of understanding. Iran's IRGC then launched ballistic missiles and drones at US military facilities in Kuwait and Bahrain. A US official confirmed no American casualties or significant damage at this time. Both sides accused the other of ceasefire violations. Iran subsequently halted diplomatic processes and threatened further retaliation. President Trump issued statements on Truth Social warning Iran of severe consequences including regime destruction.
