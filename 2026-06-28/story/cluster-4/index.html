Thunderstorms cause hundreds of flight delays and cancellations at Heathrow and Gatwick following record June heatwave

Unbiased summary

Severe thunderstorms following a record-breaking June heatwave caused significant disruption to air travel at Heathrow and Gatwick airports on Saturday. FlightRadar24 data indicated over 400 delays at Gatwick and around 383 at Heathrow, with roughly 200 cancellations across both airports. Temporary air traffic control restrictions were imposed at Gatwick due to the weather. Aviation agency Eurocontrol identified Heathrow and Gatwick as the only UK airports experiencing heavy delays. Some disruption also occurred at Edinburgh, Leeds Bradford and London City airports. Temperatures had reached a provisional peak of 37.3C at Santon Downham, Suffolk on Friday, breaking the UK June record on three consecutive days. The Met Office issued amber heat warnings and yellow thunderstorm warnings, with further storms forecast. Lightning strikes also caused house fires in Kent and East Sussex.
