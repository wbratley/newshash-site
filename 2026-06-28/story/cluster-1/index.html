England beat Panama 2-0 to top Group L; Bellingham scores, Kane breaks World Cup record, DR Congo next

Unbiased summary

England defeated Panama 2-0 in their final 2026 World Cup group game at MetLife Stadium, New Jersey, finishing top of Group L with seven points. A goalless first half gave way to second-half goals from Jude Bellingham, who converted from a Saka corner, and Harry Kane, who headed in from Bellingham's cross. Kane's goal was his 11th World Cup goal, surpassing Gary Lineker's England record of 10. England had previously beaten Croatia 4-2 and drawn 0-0 with Ghana. Tuchel acknowledged the win was hard-fought while expressing belief his side would improve in the knockouts. DR Congo, who beat Uzbekistan 3-1 in a comeback win featuring two goals from Yoane Wissa, qualified third in their group and will face England in Atlanta on 1 July.
