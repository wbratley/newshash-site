ENGLAND’S last-32 opponent and path to the World Cup final has been CONFIRMED. The Three Lions face DR Congo in the first knockout round and will likely have to overcome some of the planet’s best teams to reach the showpiece event on July 19 in New York. Jude Bellingham and Harry Kane scored second-half goals in the victory over Panama in their final group game. The win sealed their spot at the top of the group while Croatia finished second with a 2-1 success over Ghana. Finishing as group winners has given Thomas Tuchel‘s side the privilege of facing one of the eight best third-placed teams. They had to fight back against Uzbekistan after Eldor Shomurodov opened the scoring in the opening ten minutes. But goals from Yoane Wissa and Fiston Mayele were enough for DR Congo to progress.
