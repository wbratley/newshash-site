Portugal beat Croatia 2-1 to reach World Cup last 16 in match featuring four disallowed goals and late VAR intervention

Unbiased summary

Portugal defeated Croatia 2-1 in their World Cup last-32 match in Toronto. Ivan Perisic gave Croatia the lead early in the second half. Cristiano Ronaldo, 41, equalised with a penalty — his first ever goal in a World Cup knockout match — before being substituted on 81 minutes. Gonçalo Ramos headed in a stoppage-time winner. In the 103rd minute, Croatia appeared to equalise through Joško Gvardiol, but VAR disallowed the goal after microchip technology inside the Adidas Trionda ball confirmed Igor Matanovic had touched it, meaning Mario Pasalic was offside. The match featured a record four disallowed goals in total. Portugal advance to face Spain in the last 16. The match fell on the first anniversary of the death of Portuguese international Diogo Jota, who died in a car crash in July 2025. Ronaldo was controversially named player of the match.
