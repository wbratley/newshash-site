World Cup 2026 preparations underway as Ollie Robinson returns to England Test cricket with four-wicket haul at Lord's

Unbiased summary

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is approaching, with all 26-man squads now finalised across participating nations. Teams including New Zealand and Egypt have confirmed their rosters, with notable players such as Chris Wood and Mohamed Salah featuring. Off the pitch, FIFA faces criticism for failing to reimburse fans who sold tickets through its official resale marketplace, with at least one supporter owed £1,250 beyond a missed deadline. In cricket, England bowler Ollie Robinson returned to Test cricket after a 24-match absence, taking four wickets for 10 runs in six overs against New Zealand at Lord's on day one, leaving New Zealand at 61-6 and trailing England by 79 runs. Separately, Dutch police detained four men in connection with allegations of drugging and sexually abusing women and sharing videos of the abuse.
