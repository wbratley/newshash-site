Bukayo Saka in World Cup injury fear as Thomas Tuchel admits Arsenal star cannot play full 90 minutes for England

Article text

BUKAYO SAKA is a massive fitness worry ahead of the World Cup — and still not able to play 90 minutes. England boss Thomas Tuchel revealed the Arsenal winger has been struggling with his Achilles issue and cannot take part in all training sessions. Saka pulled out of Three Lions duty in March and started just five games for Arsenal after the Carabao Cup final that same month. Ahead of facing Costa Rica in a warm-up match in Orlando on Thursday, Tuchel said: “Bukayo is still getting there, playing through discomfort at the end of the season, but obviously managing it and playing at a high level. “But he is still not 100 per cent for us. Some things are missing for Bukayo in terms of consecutive training. “Arsenal took very good care of him and were very aware of it. So step-by-step, he is at the moment not able to do every training session through the week and then play. Read More on Football “I didn’t say Bukayo was not ready to start. But I think it is very unlikely Bukayo starts and finishes the matches all from now on.” England certainly need a fit and firing Saka who has been a key player for the nation at the last three major tournaments. Saka was taken off during the Champions League final and was definitely not at his best towards the end of the season, even though Arsenal won the Premier League. Tuchel claimed otherwise, though. He said: “Bukayo was very decisive. They brought him back against Fulham when the season and title was on the line and he was straight away decisive.
