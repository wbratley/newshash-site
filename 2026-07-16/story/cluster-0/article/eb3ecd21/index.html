As Bellingham moved away before play resumed, the Argentina captain produced a viral moment by staring at him and then nodding his head sarcastically. And in the end, it was Messi who had the last laugh after providing two assists to help his country come from behind to reach the World Cup final. Anthony Gordon fired England ahead in the 55th minute, turning home Morgan Rogers' cross from close range to break the deadlock. Yet almost half an hour later Enzo Fernandez drew Argentina level with a sensational long-range finish after receiving the ball from Messi, before Lautaro Martinez headed home the captain's cross in the 92nd minute to break English hearts. Argentina midfielder Leandro Paredes was also branded 'pathetic' for attempting to get England captain Harry Kane sent off in the semifinal. Midfielder Elliot Anderson was repeatedly fouled and eventually bit back, earning himself a yellow card - before Paredes launched his cynical scheme. Kane went over to the referee to discuss Anderson's booking and briefly covered his mouth while he was speaking. Paredes then instantly started complaining to the official, signalling that Kane had covered his mouth. A new FIFA rule means that players are not allowed to cover their mouths when talking to other stars - but this does not include referees.
