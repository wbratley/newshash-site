Mexico beat Ecuador 2-0 in weather-delayed World Cup last-32 match at Estadio Azteca to reach round of 16

Unbiased summary

Mexico defeated Ecuador 2-0 in a World Cup round of 32 match at the Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, with kick-off delayed approximately one hour due to thunderstorms and lightning in the vicinity. Goals from Julián Quiñones in the 22nd minute and Raúl Jiménez shortly after gave Mexico a comfortable first-half lead. Ecuador offered little response in the second half. In stoppage time, Ecuador defender Piero Hincapie was shown a straight red card following a VAR review after he covered his mouth while confronting Mexico striker Santiago Gimenez, violating a new FIFA rule. Mexico's victory ends a run of knockout-stage defeats stretching back to 1986 and sets up a potential round-of-16 clash against England or DR Congo at the same venue. There was also brief confusion over whether hydration breaks would be used, with officials initially cancelling them before reinstating them.
