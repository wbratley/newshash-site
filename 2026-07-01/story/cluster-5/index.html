Serena Williams loses Wimbledon singles comeback match to Maya Joint in three sets

Unbiased summary

Serena Williams, 44, returned to professional singles tennis at Wimbledon after a four-year absence, receiving a wild card entry to play Australia's Maya Joint, world number 87, on Centre Court. Williams last played singles at the 2022 US Open before announcing her retirement. Joint won the match 6-3, 6-7(6), 6-3 in approximately two and a half hours. Williams saved a match point in the second-set tie-break before forcing a deciding third set, but ultimately lost. Williams' serve remained effective, including a 121mph ace, but her movement was limited and she faded physically in the third set. A crowd of around 15,000 gave Williams a standing ovation on arrival. Williams' husband Alexis Ohanian, daughter Olympia, and initially daughter Adira, aged two, were present. Williams will continue at Wimbledon in the women's doubles alongside sister Venus.
