World Cup 2026 group stage concludes with Australia, Japan, Sweden, Ecuador, Netherlands and USA advancing to last 32

Unbiased summary

The World Cup 2026 group stage concluded with several simultaneous final matchday fixtures. Australia drew 0-0 with Paraguay to finish second in Group D behind USA, who lost 3-2 to Turkey in a dead-rubber match with a rotated squad. In Group F, Japan drew 1-1 with Sweden — Daizen Maeda scored for Japan and Anthony Elanga equalised for Sweden — with Netherlands beating Tunisia 3-1 to top the group; all three sides progressed. In Group E, Ecuador defeated Germany 1-0 through Gonzalo Plata's 77th-minute goal, advancing as one of the best third-placed teams, while Germany and Ivory Coast also qualified. The USA, despite losing to Turkey, had already secured top spot in Group D. Scotland's third-place hopes were damaged by a 3-0 loss to Brazil.
