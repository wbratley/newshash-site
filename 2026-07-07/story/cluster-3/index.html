Prince Harry arrives in UK for charity engagements amid confusion over Buckingham Palace accommodation and pending Daily Mail court ruling

Unbiased summary

Prince Harry arrived in the UK on Monday for a week of charity engagements supporting the Invictus Games in London and Birmingham. His wife Meghan and their children did not travel to London due to security concerns, though they may join him later in Birmingham. A dispute arose over accommodation at Buckingham Palace: Harry's team announced he had accepted an invitation to stay there, but Buckingham Palace said he had been told on Saturday he could not stay because he had not accepted the offer in time. Harry's team described the invitation as having been withdrawn at the last moment, citing the pending High Court judgment in his case against Associated Newspapers as a possible reason. The palace cited staffing and logistical constraints, and noted concerns about the legal case potentially compromising the King's constitutional position. On Tuesday, a ruling was expected in Harry's case against the Daily Mail's publisher, brought alongside Sir Elton John, Baroness Doreen Lawrence, and others.
