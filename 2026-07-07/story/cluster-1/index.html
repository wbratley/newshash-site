England beat Mexico 3-2 in World Cup last-16 tie; pubs see surge in trade; Henderson injured; Starmer intervened on kick-off time

Unbiased summary

England defeated co-host Mexico 3-2 in a World Cup round-of-16 match played at the Azteca Stadium in Mexico City, kicking off at approximately 2am UK time on Monday after lightning storms delayed the original 1am start. Jude Bellingham scored twice in the first half, Harry Kane added a penalty in the 60th minute, and Mexico pulled back two goals. England played the final 36 minutes with ten men after Jarell Quansah was sent off. England advance to a quarter-final against Norway. Prime Minister Keir Starmer intervened diplomatically to prevent FIFA from bringing forward the kick-off, which the FA argued would disadvantage England at altitude. UK pub footfall rose significantly, with over six million pints served. Jordan Henderson suffered a serious wrist injury during post-match celebrations and underwent surgery in Mexico. Donald Trump praised Harry Kane on social media.
