Preliminary hearing begins for Tyler Robinson, charged with murder of conservative activist Charlie Kirk in September 2025

Unbiased summary

A preliminary hearing began Monday in a Utah court for Tyler Robinson, 23, charged with the aggravated murder of Charlie Kirk, the 31-year-old founder of Turning Point USA, who was fatally shot on 10 September 2025 at Utah Valley University during a campus event. The hearing, presided over by District Court Judge Tony Graf, is scheduled to last the full week and will determine whether sufficient probable cause exists to send the case to trial. Prosecutors, who are seeking the death penalty, presented surveillance footage, witness testimony and other evidence. Kirk's widow Erika and his parents were present in court. Former campus police officer Chris Bagley testified as the first prosecution witness, describing witnessing the shooting and the ensuing chaos. Robinson has not yet entered a plea.
