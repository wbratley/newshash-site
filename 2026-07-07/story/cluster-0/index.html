Trump confirms he personally asked FIFA's Infantino to review Balogun red card; ban suspended, USA lose 4-1 to Belgium

Unbiased summary

US President Donald Trump confirmed he personally contacted FIFA president Gianni Infantino to request a review of striker Folarin Balogun's red card, issued during the USA's 2-0 win over Bosnia and Herzegovina. FIFA subsequently suspended Balogun's automatic one-match ban for one year on probationary terms, citing Article 27 of its disciplinary code, allowing him to play against Belgium in the last 16. FIFA's own statutes prohibit political interference in football matters. UEFA condemned the decision as 'incomprehensible and unjustifiable,' accusing FIFA of crossing 'a red line.' Belgium formally appealed; FIFA's Appeal Committee dismissed it as inadmissible without providing Belgium with a reasoned decision. Trump insisted he only asked for a review and did not tell FIFA what to do, but also called the original referee 'suspect.' Belgium won the match 4-1, with Charles De Ketelaere scoring twice. The FA separately began considering a similar appeal regarding Jarell Quansah's red card.
