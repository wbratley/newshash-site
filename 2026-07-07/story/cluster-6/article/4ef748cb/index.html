The Real Madrid forward scored a decisive second-half penalty to earn France a narrow 1-0 victory over Paraguay in Philadelphia on Saturday, but the round of 16 clash was marred by shameful and dirty tactics used by the underdogs. Going up against the tournament favourites, the Paraguayans used every trick in the book to try and provoke their opponents, repeatedly fouling, faking injury and trying to wind up Les Bleus with overly aggressive conduct. And now, Paraguay's controversial World Cup exit has taken another extraordinary turn after senator Celeste Amarilla launched a vile attack on France's captain. After Mbappe refused a handshake with Paraguayan goalkeeper Orlando Gill, she claimed: 'This brute hasn't even learned to write' before hurling racist insults at the superstar. 'You should've shown him the finger, Orlando Gill; I do it in the senate and nothing happens!!!' 'He was nervous and scared to death the whole match, like his entire team; they couldn't even score a goal, they won by a fluke... 'The only thing many of us demanded of the Albirroja is not to have slapped him open-handed after the match ended. And that's despite not being a soccer fan.' Mbappe furiously responded to her astonishing tirade with his own statement on Monday, which read: 'Madame Celeste Amarilla, You are a despicable woman and unworthy of your position. You do not represent Paraguay, that country which has sweated passion and honor throughout the competition. 'Through your recklessness and your brazen racism, the entire world has already forgotten the journey and the historic effort that your players accomplished during this World Cup, making way for an incompetent woman who gives the worst possible image of her country. 'I will never allow people like her the freedom to spread their hatred and racism across the world.' French Football Federation president Philippe Diallo also threw his support behind Mbappe and posted: 'The racist remarks by Paraguayan Senator Celeste Amarilla targeting Kylian Mbappe are criminal and reprehensible. 'They must be prosecuted here as elsewhere. The FFF is filing a report with the prosecutor's office for the purpose of judicial proceedings. Paraguay's dirty tactics included various incidents during the match, from scuffing up the penalty spot, elbowing Dayot Upamecano in the ribs, palming Jules Kounde's face, trying to get Michael Olise sent off with outrageous play-acting and somehow ending up without a single booking from Uzbekistani referee Ilgiz Tantashev. After the tempestuous match, BBC pundit Joe Hart branded Paraguay's players 'an absolute disgrace', while French newspaper L'Equipe slapped Tantashev with a brutal 1/10 rating for failing to seize control of the occasion. In a tense flashpoint of the match, goalkeeper Gill was left furious after Mbappe refused to shake his hands while celebrating in front of the French fans. Gill reacted furiously, throwing the ball at Mbappe's back before he walked over to the centre circle, where another melee took place. Speaking after the match, Gill fumed: 'Kylian Mbappe has p***ed me off. I stretched my hand to congratulate him, but he ignored me. 'This is football, if they're not used to this, what can we do? Paraguay is like that, it's a tough team. 'From the first moment we set out to make ourselves felt on the field, that if the ball gets through, the man doesn't.' Speaking about the controversial encounter, Mbappe hailed his side and said they are prepared to win via any method at this World Cup. 'We knew what kind of game we were going to have. We showed that we were not just a team that knows how to play offensive football,' he said. 'They thought we'd come wearing tuxedos, doing great plays, one-twos and whatnot. It's their way of playing.'
