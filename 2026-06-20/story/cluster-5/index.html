Starmer faces mounting pressure to resign after Burnham wins Makerfield by-election with large majority

Unbiased summary

Andy Burnham won the Makerfield by-election with approximately 55% of the vote, beating Reform UK on around 35%. Following the result, a number of Cabinet ministers including Transport Secretary Heidi Alexander, and previously Ed Miliband and Shabana Mahmood, have urged Starmer to set a timetable for his departure. Starmer has refused to resign, stating he will not 'walk away' and will fight any leadership contest, while warning against plunging the country 'into chaos'. He spent Friday calling Cabinet ministers to gauge support. Burnham is expected to travel to London on Monday to meet MPs. Some Labour figures are hoping for a coronation rather than a full contest. Starmer is reportedly spending the weekend with family considering his position. A key moment is expected at Tuesday's Cabinet meeting.
