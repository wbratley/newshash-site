Israel-Hezbollah ceasefire renewed after deadly Lebanon flare-up disrupts US-Iran nuclear talks in Switzerland

Unbiased summary

A fragile ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah was renewed on Friday after a 24-hour escalation in which Hezbollah killed four Israeli soldiers and Israel conducted airstrikes across southern Lebanon and the Bekaa valley, killing at least 47 people. The violence led Iran to cancel scheduled implementation talks with the US in Switzerland regarding a memorandum of understanding signed earlier that week, which included a provision for a ceasefire in Lebanon. JD Vance had been expected to lead the US delegation but did not travel. Trump's envoy Steve Witkoff was already in Switzerland, and Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi was reportedly planning to attend Saturday. Trump publicly stated Netanyahu 'will do as I say' while US intelligence reportedly warned Netanyahu may attempt to undermine the deal. Separately, Italian PM Giorgia Meloni publicly denied Trump's claim that she had begged him for a photo at the G7 summit.
