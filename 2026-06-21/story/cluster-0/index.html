Man charged after series of attacks across Edinburgh leaves five injured; counter-terrorism police investigate suspected anti-Muslim motive

Unbiased summary

On the evening of Friday 19 June 2026, Police Scotland responded to a fast-moving series of violent incidents across Edinburgh beginning at approximately 8:50pm. Two men were first injured in the Sighthill area near Broomhouse Mosque, with further incidents reported around retailers in the west and north of the city, including on Telford Road and Leith Walk. Five men in total were injured — two aged 22 and others aged 24, 27, and 39 — three of whom required hospital treatment; none suffered life-threatening injuries. A 36-year-old white Scottish man was detained at around 9:30pm and subsequently charged. Counter-terrorism officers were brought in to assist the investigation. Muslim community organisations stated several victims were Muslim. Prime Minister Keir Starmer and Scottish First Minister John Swinney both publicly condemned the attacks. Police confirmed no further threat to the public.
