Harry Kane double rescues England against DR Congo; Belgium, USA also reach World Cup last 16 in dramatic fashion

Unbiased summary

England beat DR Congo 2-1 in Atlanta to reach the World Cup last 16. DR Congo's Brian Cipenga scored in the seventh minute, and England struggled to break down goalkeeper Lionel Mpasi for most of the match. Harry Kane equalised with a header from Anthony Gordon's cross in the 75th minute and scored a second in the 86th minute to seal victory. Kane was denied a penalty claim near half-time; VAR upheld the referee's no-penalty decision. England will face Mexico in the round of 16. In other matches, Belgium came from 2-0 down to beat Senegal 3-2 in extra time, with a contentious VAR-awarded penalty in the 125th minute settling the tie. The USA beat Bosnia and Herzegovina 2-0, with Folarin Balogun scoring before receiving a controversial straight red card, and Malik Tillman adding a late free kick.
