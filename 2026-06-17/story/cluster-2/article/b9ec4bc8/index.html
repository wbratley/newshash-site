10 best pubs to watch England vs Croatia game in London with hidden gem on the list

Article text

Need to know 10 best pubs to watch England vs Croatia game in London with hidden gem on the list England fans who couldn't get tickets for the World Cup in North America may well find themselves on the lookout for a pub to watch the Three Lions' opener Two England fans discuss their World Cup journey from Kansas to Dallas Here's everything you need to know about where to watch England vs Croatia in London: - The Faltering Fullback (N4): The Faltering Fullback is an iconic North London favourite renowned for its multi-tiered garden and Thai food. This charming venue offers an atmospheric setting to watch football, but you will need to get there early to secure a spot since they operate on a strict first-come, first-served basis without tickets. - Prince of Peckham (SE15): The Prince of Peckham Pub will be serving up brilliant Caribbean-inspired food and screening matches throughout the World Cup. They also have an all-day happy hour on Wednesday with two-for-one on selected cocktails. - The Kings Arms (E2): A traditional East London boozer, the Kings Arms is showing England and Scotland matches and it's walk-ins only. Anyone feeling peckish during the game can order pizzas from Yard Sale directly to the pub. - The Volley (EC1V) The Volley serves as a dedicated hub for passionate football culture, offering an alternative and less touristy vibe than standard sports bars. It is now walk-in only and the small capacity means you may want to get down early to guarantee a spot. - Nordic Bar (W1T): Nordic Bar provides a special underground setting to watch England games right in the middle of London. As the name suggests, it could also serve as a great spot for Norway and Sweden matches. - Pub On The Park (E8): Pub On The Park borders London Fields and boasts one of the most popular and expansive beer gardens in the East End. They are operating a walk-ins only policy for England matches so you may need to get down early. - The Drayton Court Hotel (W13): The Drayton Court Hotel is famous for housing one of the largest pub gardens in the entire city, offering plenty of breathing room for fans. Entry to the main garden areas is generally free on a first-come basis, but tickets are required for England games with £15 getting you entry and a free drink - Kruk (SE15): Kruk is a Thai restaurant in South-East London which is turning into a destination for England matches during the World Cup. Tickets cost £5 per person and they will be serving BBQ food, cold beers and spritzes for those in attendance. - Feed the Yak (SE17): Located in Elephant & Castle, Feed the Yak is a dedicated football bar with a selection of craft beers. In addition to the England matches, it could serve as a hidden gem for watching other games during the tournament. - Five Points Brewery Taproom (E8): The Five Points Brewery Taproom and Courtyard features a massive open-air setup with indoor-outdoor HD screens ideal for summer tournament viewing.
