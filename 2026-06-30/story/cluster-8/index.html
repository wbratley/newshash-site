Former Conservative MP Craig Williams pleads guilty to using insider knowledge to bet on 2024 general election date

Unbiased summary

Craig Williams, 41, a former Conservative MP who served as Rishi Sunak's parliamentary private secretary from October 2022 to June 2024, pleaded guilty at Southwark Crown Court to cheating at gambling. Williams admitted using confidential information gained from attending planning meetings at Downing Street and Conservative headquarters to place bets of £250, £100, and £22.50 on the date of the 2024 general election. Three additional cheating charges he denied will be dropped at sentencing. Sentencing is delayed pending co-defendants' trials scheduled for 2025 and 2028. Amy Hind, 35, wife of Conservative deputy digital director Anthony Hind, also pleaded guilty to cheating at gambling, having placed bets of £10, £5, and £20 on the election date. Williams had previously described his actions as a 'huge error of judgment' when the story broke in June 2024.
