US Supreme Court expands presidential firing power over most agencies but protects Federal Reserve independence in split rulings

Unbiased summary

The US Supreme Court issued multiple significant rulings on the same day. In Trump v Slaughter, the court overturned nearly 90 years of precedent by ruling that the president can remove heads of independent federal agencies without cause, a decision stemming from Trump's firing of FTC Commissioner Rebecca Slaughter. Separately, in a 5-4 decision, the court blocked Trump from firing Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, upholding the Fed's statutory protections against politically motivated removals. Chief Justice Roberts and Justice Kavanaugh joined the three liberal justices in the Cook majority. The court also declined to hear Trump's appeal of the $5 million civil judgment against him in the E. Jean Carroll sexual abuse and defamation case, meaning that verdict stands. The rulings collectively represent a mixed outcome for presidential power claims.
