Paraguay beat Germany 4-3 on penalties after 1-1 draw to reach World Cup last 16 in Boston

Unbiased summary

Paraguay eliminated Germany from the 2026 World Cup in the Round of 32 at Boston's Gillette Stadium, winning 4-3 on penalties after a 1-1 draw following extra time. Julio Enciso opened the scoring for Paraguay in the first half; Kai Havertz equalised for Germany after the break. In extra time, Jonathan Tah headed what appeared to be a winner for Germany, but VAR ruled the goal out after Waldemar Anton was judged to have fouled goalkeeper Orlando Gill. In the shootout, Havertz and Nick Woltemade missed for Germany, while Antonio Sanabria and Fabián Balbuena missed for Paraguay before Jose Canale scored the winning penalty. It was Germany's first ever World Cup shootout defeat. Manuel Neuer subsequently announced his retirement from international football, and manager Julian Nagelsmann faces uncertainty over his future.
