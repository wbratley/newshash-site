UK government proposes £10,000 repayment charge for asylum seekers under new Immigration and Asylum Bill

Unbiased summary

The UK government has announced plans to require asylum seekers to repay approximately £10,000 towards their state-funded accommodation and support costs, as part of the upcoming Immigration and Asylum Bill to be put before Parliament. The means-tested scheme, which applies only to adults with sufficient funds, would be paid in monthly instalments and must be cleared before applicants become eligible for Indefinite Leave to Remain. Children are exempt. Home Secretary Shabana Mahmood framed the policy as balancing rights with responsibilities, noting the government has already cut asylum costs by £1 billion from a £4 billion annual spend. Experts caution that fewer than 15% of refugees earn above £20,000 five years after being granted asylum, limiting expected revenue. Refugee charities have condemned the plan as unfair and harmful to integration.

Bias The Guardian leads with charity criticism and includes the strongest language from opponents, including the phrase 'performative cruelty'. It downplays logistical details such as the means-testing safeguards, the exemption for children, and the prospective-only application of the law, which are presented more fully in centrist outlets.
