Explosion injures three in Monaco residential building, suspect flees to France as manhunt launched

Unbiased summary

On Monday evening, 29 June 2025, at approximately 9pm local time, an explosion occurred at a residential building on Rue Révérend Père Louis Frolla in Monaco, near the French border. CCTV footage showed a man in a black bucket hat leaving a rucksack in the building's entrance hall before fleeing on foot toward the French town of Beausoleil. Two adults, a couple believed to be in their 50s or 60s, were critically injured and transported to Pasteur Hospital in Nice; a 13-year-old, likely related to the couple, sustained less serious injuries. The victims were reported to be Ukrainian, with one identified as businessman Vadim Ermolaev. The device reportedly contained bolts and buckshot. Monaco's Minister of State Christophe Mirmand described it as a deliberate act, calling it unprecedented in the principality's history. A police manhunt is ongoing.

Coverage by outlet

Instead, it spends editorial space characterising Monaco as a billionaire tax haven, which is tangential to the incident. It also includes Éric Ciotti's quote but identifies him primarily as 'rightwing', an editorial label not applied to other sources quoted, suggesting mild political framing.
