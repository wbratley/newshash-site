England vs Mexico kick-off remains at 1am BST after FIFA reverses plan to reschedule due to storm concerns

Unbiased summary

FIFA considered moving England's World Cup last-16 tie against Mexico in Mexico City from 18:00 local time (01:00 BST Monday) to 12:00 local time (19:00 BST Sunday), citing forecasted thunderstorms and potential flooding near the Azteca Stadium. News of the proposed change leaked via Mexican media on Friday afternoon, catching both the English FA and Mexican FA off guard. Both associations pushed back against the change, raising concerns about player preparation, fan travel logistics, and the operational challenges of reorganising a matchday operation involving over 50,000 personnel. After approximately five and a half hours of discussions between FIFA and multiple stakeholders, FIFA confirmed the original kick-off time would stand. Commercial and broadcasting considerations, including scheduling conflicts with the Brazil vs Norway tie, were also reported as factors. The game will be broadcast live in the UK on the BBC.

Bias The Guardian provides substantive detail and largely sticks to the facts, but editorially frames FIFA's handling as 'farcical' and emphasises the FA being 'less than impressed,' lending a tone of institutional critique. The coverage is among the more thorough but subtly positions FIFA as the villain of the episode rather than maintaining strict neutrality.
