King's Birthday Honours 2026 recognises 1,182 people including Kevin Sinfield, Helen Mirren, six Lionesses and politicians from multiple parties

Unbiased summary

The King's Birthday Honours 2026 list recognised 1,182 people across British society. Rugby league player Kevin Sinfield was knighted for fundraising over £11 million for motor neurone disease charities, inspired by late teammate Rob Burrow. Actress Helen Mirren and photojournalist Don McCullin were made Companions of Honour. Six England Lionesses, including Chloe Kelly and Alessia Russo, received MBEs following their Euro 2025 victory. Authors Malorie Blackman and Julia Donaldson were made Dames. Political honours included a damehood for Labour MP Jessica Morden, knighthoods for former Conservative MPs David Gauke and Greg Hands, and a knighthood for political diarist Chris Mullin. Civil servant Sarah Munby, previously connected to the Horizon scandal controversy, received a CB. Animation studio founders Peter Lord and David Sproxton were also knighted.

Coverage by outlet

Angle The Guardian foregrounds feel-good community and sporting achievements, particularly celebrating the Lionesses and Sinfield, while giving relatively warm coverage to all recipients.
Bias The Guardian leads with Helen Mirren and the Lionesses, emphasising Mirren's recent street harassment incident to generate sympathy and context around her honour. It gives prominent space to a grassroots community volunteer, reinforcing a narrative about ordinary people being honoured. Political honours — including the damehood for Starmer ally Jessica Morden and the controversial CB for Sarah Munby — are entirely omitted from the covered excerpts, avoiding any potentially uncomfortable framing for a left-leaning readership.
