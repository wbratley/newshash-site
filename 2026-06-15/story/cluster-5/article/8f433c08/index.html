London Gatwick live: Passengers stranded at airport after sinkhole with 'all trains cancelled'

Article text

London Gatwick live: Passengers stranded at airport after sinkhole with 'all trains cancelled' Sink holes have opened up causing chaos for rail passengers this afternoon as train lines were closed between Purley and East Croydon in London with the Gatwick Express also suspended Passengers have been left stranded at Gatwick Airport in London after train lines were closed after sink holes were discovered. The shocking development has shut all train lines between Purley and East Croydon, throwing many into transport chaos this afternoon. The Gatwick Express is also suspended and isn't expected to run until Monday morning as baffled passengers were seen waiting at the airport. Key Events Trains are now running normally following urgent repairs to a bridge at Purley, according to National Rail. National Highways said in a statement: "Due to urgent repairs on rail network, expect increased traffic M23 west spur towards Gatwick Airport. "All lanes open A23 west towards North Terminal after Airport Way Roundabout E Delays have now cleared." Lines have reopened following urgent repairs to a bridge at Purley - but National Rail is still urging people not to use the service. This is because fewer trains than normal are able to run through Purley station and as a result, trains may still be cancelled or delayed. A spokesperson said : "You are advised not to travel. Major disruption is expected until the end of service." In its latest update, Network Rail said its engineers "have carried out thorough inspections on the bridge and have worked to plug the sinkholes, allowing trains to safely run again". "Though trains are now running we do expect there to be disruption into the night so we’re urging passengers to check before travelling," they also said. They added: "Sinkholes were spotted on the bridge, meaning that ballast, the stones that the track sits on, was falling through the gap, making the track unstable."
