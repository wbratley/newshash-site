Haaland won the contest, and the day, and wrote his name across a small Scandinavian nation's football history. When he rose with his decisive winning header, having strutted and skulked around Brazil's box for 80 minutes with vanishingly few opportunities, Haaland strolled away, suppressing the urge to smile or laugh or scream into the brilliant blue New Jersey sky. But the look behind his eyes said enough. For Norway, it delivered the stand-out result of this World Cup: the 21st ranked nation on the planet beating a fifth-ranked team oozing World Cup history. They play the winners of England and Mexico in Miami. For Haaland, it was an almighty blow against Gabriel, who rose for that lofted ball with him and was left floundering on the ground. The Brazilian knew. He punched the turf in anger. Respect to Vini Jr for not remonstrating with substitute Endrick for a scandalous waste of the game's stand-out chance, just before the hour mark. Vini danced inside Kristoffer Ajer and took out three more shirts with his arced pass. Endrick's touch was desultory. The Gabriel and Erling show Gabriel watched Haaland like a hawk, never switching his attention to another Norwegian forward for more than a moment. As the first half reached its conclusion, Haaland seemed to have had the beating of the Arsenal player. But Gabriel immediately reached into a huge recovering challenge. Here were the best in the world, testing each other to the limit. When Andreas Schjelderup's cross came in for the next big chance on 79 minutes, Haaland didn't miss. Vini can be too one-footed at times and that impacted his capacity to create on the several moments when he escaped Ajer and eased along the dead ball line. He has said he longs to be loved by the Brazilian public but in the final reckoning there was not enough for him to wrest the ball his nation's way. Norway had twice as many passes as Brazil and that dominance of the ball ultimately told. Super sub Norway had looked to Antonio Nusa, the 21-year-old RB Leipzig winger, to lay waste to an ageing Brazilian defence as he had the Japanese defence in the Houston round of 32. Stale Solbakken was concerned enough to withdraw the winger at half-time and introduce Andreas Schjelderup, the Benfica player, on the left. Schjelderup proved much more decisive than any of the Brazil substitutes before carving out the opener.
