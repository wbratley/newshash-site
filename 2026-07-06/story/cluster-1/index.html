FIFA lifts Balogun ban for USA vs Belgium after Trump lobbying; Belgium protests decision citing tournament regulations

Unbiased summary

FIFA's disciplinary committee suspended US striker Folarin Balogun's one-game ban, imposed after a red card in the USA's win over Bosnia and Herzegovina, allowing him to play in the last-16 clash against Belgium. FIFA cited Article 27 of its disciplinary code, which permits suspension of red cards not related to match-fixing, placing Balogun on a one-year probationary period. Reports indicate US President Donald Trump made multiple calls to FIFA lobbying for the reversal. Trump publicly thanked FIFA on Truth Social. The Royal Belgian Football Association said it was 'astonished', argued the reversal contradicted explicit World Cup 2026 regulations and a May 2026 circular reaffirming automatic bans, and said it was investigating all options. Belgium coach Rudi Garcia compared the decision to an April Fools' joke. FIFA noted precedent existed, having previously used Article 27 to clear Cristiano Ronaldo. Separately, England defeated Mexico 3-2 at the Azteca in a last-16 match played at approximately 2am BST following a storm delay.
