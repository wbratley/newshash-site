Ten-man England beat Mexico 3-2 at Azteca to reach World Cup quarter-finals, with Bellingham brace and Kane penalty

Unbiased summary

England defeated co-host Mexico 3-2 in the World Cup round of 16 at the Estadio Azteca, Mexico City, after a one-hour delay due to thunderstorms. Jude Bellingham scored twice in 98 seconds late in the first half before Julian Quiñones pulled one back for Mexico before half-time. In the second half, Jarell Quansah was shown a straight red card following a VAR review for a high challenge on Jesus Gallardo. Harry Kane then converted a penalty to make it 3-1 after Anthony Gordon was fouled by goalkeeper Raul Rangel. Mexico reduced the deficit to 3-2 when Raul Jiménez scored a penalty after Kane fouled Brian Gutierrez, confirmed by VAR. England held on through 11 minutes of stoppage time in a 5-3-1 formation. Manager Thomas Tuchel publicly criticised the officials post-match. Jordan Henderson suffered a wrist injury during post-match celebrations and was taken to hospital. England face Norway in the quarter-finals.
