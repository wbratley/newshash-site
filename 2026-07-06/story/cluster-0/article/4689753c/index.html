After the one hour delay to kick-off due to the weather, Jude Bellingham stole the show in the first half, with two goals in just over a minute putting the Three Lions in the ascendency against the co-hosts. Julian Quinones reduced the lead before the break however, his strike crashed into the roof of Jordan Pickford's net. Pickford had earlier twice brilliantly denied Raul Jimenez. Into the second half, England looked to take back control, but Jarell Quansah - in the side at right-back tonight - was sent off for a horror tackle. Within minutes however, Kane blasted home to score from the penalty spot after Anthony Gordon had been brought down, and made it 3-1. Moments later, Kane conceded a penalty at the other end - given after VAR intervention - and Raul Jimenez found the back of the net. Down to 10 men, England went all out defence, 5-3-1, and were resolute, dogged, brave - and booked their spot in the last eight, where they will face Norway, after the brilliant and ruthless Erling Haaland scored twice to end Carlo Ancelotti's hopes of leading Brazil to glory this summer.
