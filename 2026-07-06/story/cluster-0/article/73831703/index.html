England's win over Mexico as it happened after brilliance from Kane and Bellingham Stay up to date with our coverage of England's last-16 tie Don't Miss Most Read Trending on GB News England kept their World Cup dream alive with a pulsating 3-2 victory over Mexico, producing a display full of quality and resilience to book their place in the quarter-finals. Thomas Tuchel's side will now face Norway after coming through one of the most demanding tests of the tournament so far. There was an added layer of drama before a ball had even been kicked, with severe thunderstorms forcing organisers to delay the match by an hour. When play eventually got underway, England quickly settled into their rhythm despite the deafening atmosphere generated by a packed Azteca Stadium. Jude Bellingham proved the difference during a sensational spell before half-time. The midfielder struck twice in less than two minutes, punishing Mexico with ruthless efficiency as England seized control of proceedings in front of a stunned home crowd. Jordan Pickford ensured that advantage remained intact with a pair of excellent saves to keep out Raul Jimenez. The Everton goalkeeper was eventually beaten shortly before the interval when Julian Quinones drilled home, giving Mexico renewed belief heading into the second half. England's task became considerably harder after the restart when Jarell Quansah received a straight red card for a high challenge on Jesus Gallardo. With the hosts enjoying a numerical advantage, the momentum swung heavily in Mexico's favour as the volume inside the stadium intensified. Rather than retreat, England found another crucial breakthrough. Anthony Gordon was fouled inside the penalty area by Raul Rangel, leaving Harry Kane to confidently send the goalkeeper the wrong way from the spot and restore his team's breathing space. The drama was far from over, however. Kane was later adjudged to have fouled Brian Gutierrez inside the area, allowing Jimenez to convert Mexico's second penalty of the evening and set up a tense finale as England clung to their slender lead. Fortunately, they held on to reign supreme. Goodnight! All the pre-match talk centred on the altitude, the hostile atmosphere and Mexico's formidable record at the Azteca. But the Three Lions stood tall, soaked up the pressure and ground out a famous victory. Chances were few and far between - but England took them when it mattered most. Thomas Tuchel: 'We are here to write our own chapters' England boss Thomas Tuchel said his side "are here to write our own chapters" ahead of tonight's clash against Mexico. The German said: "We are in an iconic place, an iconic stadium, a massive game, a knockout game against Mexico at the Azteca. "It's an iconic match and a big stage, and we feel it." Tuchel acknowledged the challenging environment, including the altitude England will be playing at and the stadium's history, with the Three Lions being knocked out of the 1986 World Cup thanks to the "Hand of God". He continued: "The players felt it in the first minutes of the training session, and the longer it went, they could cope with it better. It's just what it is. "I don't expect a hostile environment. I think it will be emotional and full of support for the home nation. Altitude, it is what it is. Home crowd, it is what it is. "We need to overcome obstacles, but we have the spirit, the commitment, the pure will and the glue in the team to overcome these things. "We're not here for revenge. We are here to write our own chapters. We are in good spirits, and we are ready to go." How did we get here? Harry Kane has been instrumental for the Three Lions | REUTERSHarry Kane is 95 per cent the reason England are in the last-16, but they still needed to make it through the group. The Three Lions kicked off their World Cup campaign on June 17 against Croatia with a 4-2 victory. Heading into half-time at 2-2 after Kane scored twice, a couple of goals from Jude Bellingham and Marcus Rashford got England off to the perfect start. Game two saw Thomas Tuchel's side play Ghana, in an uninspiring 0-0, which has quickly been erased from the memory bank by several England supporters. Panama in game three proved better, with a 2-0 win thanks to second-half goals from Bellingham and Kane. Seven points out of nine saw England finish top of their group, setting up a tie against DR Congo, who frustrated fans, going 1-0 up inside seven minutes. With 15 minutes to play, up stepped Captain Kane, with a sensational brace to set-up tonight's contest against Mexico. Mexican authorities to deploy 17,000 police officers Mexican authorities said that 17,000 police officers will be deployed for the contest tonight, according to the New York Times. There has been a heavy police presence outside England's hotel after some Mexican fans tried to disrupt the squad. The UK's ambassador to Mexico, Susannah Goshko, urged England supporters to read the Foreign Office's travel advice before attending the game and to remain aware of their surroundings. The Three Lions are set to take on the co-hosts in Mexico City, with kick-off scheduled for 1am. According to the latest forecast from Mexico's national weather service, storms are expected to begin around 4pm local time, two hours before the scheduled start.
