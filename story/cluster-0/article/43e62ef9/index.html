Who's in and out? Andy Burnham appoints Wes Streeting as defence secretary: Recap

Article text

Andy Burnham has wasted no time reshaping the Government, bringing in a host of allies and making sweeping changes to Keir Starmer's former Cabinet on his first day as Prime Minister. Wes Streeting has made a dramatic return to Cabinet as Defence Secretary after quitting Mr Starmer's government, while former defence chief John Healey has been handed the role of Chancellor. Ed Miliband has been moved to Foreign Secretary, with Shabana Mahmood remaining as Home Secretary, while a string of senior Labour figures have been promoted, moved or axed in a major Downing Street overhaul. Including former transport secretary Louise Haigh, who has been brought in as Chancellor to the Duchy of Lancaster. Pat McFadden has retained his position as Work and Pensions Secretary, Lisa Nandy has been kept on as Culture Secretary, and Yvette Cooper has been shuffled to Health Secretary. Angela Rayner has also returned to Cabinet as the Housing Secretary, and Jonathan Reynolds has been appointed as Business Secretary. While Deputy Labour leader Lucy Powell has been appointed Education Secretary - replacing Bridget Phillipson, who has been demoted to Minister for Women and Equalities. Key Starmer allies Rachel Reeves, the former chancellor, and David Lammy, the Justice Secretary, Deputy Prime Minister and one-time Foreign Secretary, both signalled they will not be serving in Burnham's Cabinet.
