England fans spill out onto the streets after packing out pubs across the country to watch Jude Bellingham carry Three Lions into World Cup semi-final

Article text

- See more Daily Mail on Google - save us as a Preferred Source Fans spilled out onto the streets in jubilant scenes after Jude Bellingham carried England into the World Cup semi finals with a 2-1 victory over Norway. Huge crowds filled London's Piccadilly in the early hours of Sunday morning with supporters seen climbing on statues and throwing pints into the air. Others were seen mocking the Norwegians' Viking row celebration by sitting en masse in the street. There appeared to be some arrests as police clashed with fans who had been partying hard in pubs and fan zones where the game was being screened. England needed extra time to secure victory over Norway in Miami and a place in the final four of the World Cup. They will now take on the winners of Argentina against Switzerland at 8pm on Wednesday. There were tense scenes in London, Newcastle and Bristol at the 90-minute mark when both teams were level at 1-1 and the game headed into extra time. But fans jumped onto tables and sent their beer flying when Bellingham scored for England. At one sold-out venue in Times Square, Newcastle, supporters began chanting: 'Don't take me home, please don't take me home, I just don't want to go to work.' Nevertheless, the loudest roar of the evening came in the 110th minute when Dan Burn was subbed on. The Magpies defender was born and raised in nearby Blyth. Delirious scenes had been cut short in the first half of the match when Harry Kane snuck one into the back of the net but the goal was disallowed for offside. There was also nervousness when Norway took their turn to go 2-1 in front but their goal was also scrubbed off the board following a VAR call. Bars were given permission to stay open until 2am - with 42 million pints expected to have been downed this evening. WeAreFanZones manager in Newcastle, Briony Young, 32, said: 'It's always busy for every England game but today's sold out in record time. 'Even though it's a bit drizzly and overcast, a little bit of rain won't put us Geordies off. 'There's been a real party atmosphere in the city all day. 'Everyone is confident that we can get the job done and march into the semi-final.'
