Tyson Fury agrees Dana White deal after flying to UFC White House

Article text

Tyson Fury agrees Dana White deal after flying to UFC White House Tyson Fury flew to Washington DC and revealed he has an announcement to make with UFC CEO Dana White Tyson Fury has teased an announcement with UFC CEO Dana White ahead of his fight with Anthony Joshua. Fury flew to the White House on Sunday to take his seat cageside for the Freedom 250 UFC event staged on the front lawn. Fury made his entrance after the second fight of the night and is expected to reveal he has signed with White's Zuffa Boxing promotional outfit ahead of his clash with Joshua which is pencilled in for November. And Fury, who was visiting the US for the first time since 2021, said: "It's been a long time and boy oh boy am I happy to be here at this historic event, freedom at the White House and Donald Trump's birthday." Asked about an announcement with White, Fury added: "I think I'm going to let Dana do the speaking, let him do his announcement but it's a massive, massive announcement." And of his fight with Joshua, Fury said: "I'm very, very excited for this fight it's been over a decade in the making and for whatever reason it hasn't happened before so let's hope it happens right now, very soon." Fury could have a warm-up fight before taking on Joshua with White due to take his promotional company to Dublin on August 8. And Fury added: "I have a potential fight in August; I'm looking forward to fighting whoever they put in front of me and getting back to what I do best, winning." White has been rowing with Joshua's promoter Eddie Hearn who insists the American has nothing to do with the all-British clash, yet Fury's trip Stateside would suggest otherwise. No date or venue has been announced for the clash but Wembley Stadium is the frontrunner for the autumn meeting. Joshua must first beat Kristian Prenga in Saudi Arabia next month while Fury will also have to come through his warm-up bout if the domestic showdown is to finally materialise. Fury flew to Washington DC on a private jet having flown from Thailand to Manchester to support brother Tommy in his fight with Eddie Hall on Saturday night. Fury also walked rapper Jordan McCann for his wild bout with Ibiza Final Boss.
