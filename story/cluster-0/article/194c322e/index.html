Andy Burnham latest: Angela Rayner returns in cabinet reshuffle as Wes Streeting handed defence secretary

Andy Burnham latest: Angela Rayner returns in cabinet reshuffle as Wes Streeting handed defence secretary John Healey was announced as chancellor in a shock move for the prime minister - Bookmark - CommentsGo to comments Angela Rayner has returned to government as housing secretary after resigning over her tax affairs last year. Wes Streeting will also return as defence secretary as part of Andy Burnham’s cabinet reshuffle, with Ed Miliband securing a place in his top team as foreign secretary and Shabana Mahmood remaining as home secretary. It comes hours after John Healey was announced as chancellor in a surprise move for the prime minister. Louise Haigh, who managed Mr Burnham’s Makerfield campaign, has been rewarded for her loyalty with a job at the heart of government as first secretary of state and chancellor of the Duchy of Lancaster. Meanwhile, chancellor Rachel Reeves, deputy prime minister David Lammy and housing secretary Steve Reed, all allies of Sir Keir Starmer, were sacked by the former mayor of Greater Manchester and have returned to the backbenches. The new prime minister earlier delivered his first speech at Downing Street, in which he said he would bring “life’s essentials back under stronger public control” to make them more affordable for the public and pledged to end rough sleeping. Concluding the address, he said: “I will give this my all, and I ask you all to pull with me. Andrew Burnham - who took over as prime minister promising a “circuit breaker” for British politics - has started in post with a bang. The new PM conducted a brutal purge of Starmer loyalists, sacking more than nine ministers who had close ties with his predecessor. David Lammy, Darren Jones, Rachel Reeves and Peter Kyle all left the government, while others - including John Healey - had shock promotions.
