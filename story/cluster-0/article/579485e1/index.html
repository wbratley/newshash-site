The MetLife Stadium has been criticised throughout this summer's tournament and once again appeared dry and patchy as Spain took on Argentina tonight. A number of instances saw stars, including the great Lionel Messi, struggle to control the ball as it bobbled along the uneven surface. And speaking during ITV's half-time coverage of the clash, Wright didn't hold any punches while assessing the venue for the biggest game in the footballing calendar. He said: 'For a World Cup final this pitch is ridiculous, it's poor, it (the game) shouldn't be on it. 'I think Spain are playing and showing how good they are because even on a bobbly, dry pitch like this, they're doing really good stuff. It's only a matter of time before they take one of these chances.' The 80,000-capacity MetLife sits on the outskirts of New York, which was hit by severe thunderstorms causing flooding in the hours ahead of kick-off in the sweltering east coast sunshine. Yet the pitch had been deemed subpar by players and managers long before it was hit by rainfall. The turf predominantly consists of natural grass, but it has been installed over an artificial surface which can make it 'dry out' quickly when temperatures soar. Brazil's Vinicius Jr was one of the stars who expressed his annoyance earlier in the tournament, telling reporters after his country's 1-1 draw with Morocco: 'Because of the weather and the heat, the grass dries out quickly and the game ends up being very slow. 'We can't build up a rhythm. That makes things difficult because we want to move the ball from one side to the other and this disrupts our game.' French coach Didier Deschamps later criticised the venue, saying: 'The pitch in New York was really tough - it took a lot out of the players' muscles.' Wright's comments came moments before the broadcaster's coverage cut to the highly-anticipated half-time show, which was facilitated by a controversial interval of 30 minutes. A star-studded half-time show featured performances from Shakira (pictured) and Justin Bieber Madonna kickstarted the performance with her hit 'Music', walking out onto the field with 2002 World Cup winners and Brazilian legends Ronaldo and Ronaldinho. Bieber was then introduced by Ted Lasso for a short, slow acoustic performance while a montage of the tournament was playing on the big screens around the 80,000-seater stadium. Shakira later returned and injected some energy into the show before the performance came to an end and the preparation for the second half quickly restarted. Yet ITV's panel of pundits were left less than impressed - nor was Wayne Rooney on the BBC. Keane said: 'A bit of anti-climax but I think the people in the stadium probably enjoyed it more than us. It was nice to see Shakira.' Wright added: '(Justin) Bieber should have done something a bit more upbeat to liven us up a bit.' While Rooney reflected bluntly: 'My favourite bit of the half time show was when it finished… I like all of them artists but I thought it was crap.' A host of stars flooded to New Jersey for the climax of this summer's tournament. Before kick-off, Top Gun and Mission Impossible superstar Tom Cruise took to the pitch to give a motivational pre-match speech. Meanwhile, President Donald Trump was pictured sat in his luxury suite next to FIFA President Gianni Infantino. Celebrity couple Timothee Chalament and Kylie Jenner were also snapped in the stands, as were Mick Jagger, Pharrell Williams, Victoria Beckham and Will Ferrell. Despite the substandard quality of the surface, Spain ultimately ran out victorious on the east coast thanks to a late goal from Ferran Torres. Nico Williams nodded a header down to the Barcelona man with the scores level in the 106th minute, and he fired an effort into the roof of the net sparking jubilant scenes both in the US and back in Madrid. The South Americans applied pressure of their own in search of a goal late on and Tottenham Hotspur defender Marcos Senesi almost levelled from close range. But, in the end, it wasn't to be for Messi on what could his final appearance on football's biggest stage as Lamine Yamal and his Spanish team-mates held on for a historic victory. Joe Hart said on the BBC: 'They were never rattled or under pressure. They haven't nibbled once. They played beautiful football the entire time. 'Yamal, at 19 years old, his confidence, the ways that he's doing this. Everyone will be trying to get on his back and hate on him, but he's got a Euros and World Cup. He kept so cool, kept his head, and had those standout moments.' The match was, however, at times overshadowed by the antics of the Argentinians. After taunting England's stars in Atlanta on Wednesday, Argentina's behaviour against Spain was dubbed 'embarrassing' by pundits after the final whistle tonight. Chelsea midfielder Enzo Fernandez saw red in the 90+3-minute for a late challenge on Pau Cabarsi before chaotic scenes ensured when Spain's win was confirmed. As the Spanish bench descended onto the pitch to celebrate, Argentinian substitute Leandro Paredes took issue with Barcelona midfielder Gavi. The pair became embroiled in a row, causing Scaloni to intervene, before ex-Juventus midfielder Paredes appeared to direct two punches at Gavi as he fell to the turf. And, speaking on the BBC, ex-Man United striker Wayne Rooney was scathing in his criticism of Argentina's behaviour, not only on Sunday, but throughout the entire tournament. He said: 'It doesn't surprise me. We've seen this from Argentina before. It's just sad, very poor from them. If you lose a game of football, be graceful. It's a reaction to them being outplayed.
