UK Prison Early Release Scheme Paused

Unbiased summary

The UK government has paused a prison early release scheme due to start in September, following criticism from victims' families and concerns over public safety. The scheme, intended to ease prison overcrowding, would have allowed some prisoners to be released after serving a third of their sentence. Prime Minister Andy Burnham announced the pause, citing the need for an urgent review to minimize risk to the public. The decision comes after the widow of PC Andrew Harper, who was killed in the line of duty, spoke out against the scheme, describing it as 'deplorable'. The government will conduct a review to assess the scheme's impact on public safety and consider changes.
