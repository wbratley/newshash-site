Emma Raducanu withdraws from Wimbledon after scan confirms stress fracture in right leg on eve of tournament

Unbiased summary

Emma Raducanu, seeded 30th, withdrew from Wimbledon on the eve of the tournament after a scan confirmed a stress fracture in her lower right leg. She announced the decision on Instagram shortly after 22:00 BST on Sunday 28 June, approximately seven hours after telling journalists she intended to play. Raducanu had been managing the injury for several weeks, was spotted in a protective boot on Wednesday, missed training Thursday and Friday, returned to limited practice Saturday and Sunday, but received medical advice to stop after a final scan Sunday evening. She had been scheduled to face Croatia's Antonia Ruzic on Court One on Monday. The withdrawal extends a difficult period for Raducanu, who had also dealt with foot problems, a virus, and post-viral illness earlier in the season.
