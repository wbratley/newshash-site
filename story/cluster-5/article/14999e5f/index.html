Thomas Tuchel set to rip up England’s World Cup routine if they beat Congo after nightmare draw

Article text

THOMAS TUCHEL will change England’s World Cup routine if they beat DR Congo on Wednesday. The Three Lions will train today and tomorrow in Kansas before flying to Atlanta for the last 32 clash which has a noon kick-off time – 5pm British Summer Time. Yet it is for a potential clash with hosts Mexico or Ecuador at the famous Azteca Stadium where Tuchel is set to make a switch. Tuchel will look to avoid the heartbreak from England’s 1986 quarter-final at the Azteca, where England were booted out by Diego Maradona’s Argentina. That night, 40 years ago, Argentina won 2-1 following the much-debated “Hand of God” incident. This round of 16 clash would be staged on Sunday night – in the early hours of Monday morning in the UK – but the squad would travel on Friday, a day earlier than normal. Mexico City is at altitude so there are certain considerations ahead of the tie. The England base in Kansas City has been fitted with hyperbaric chambers so players can breathe pure oxygen to supercharge recovery. The squad have also been decked out in hi-tech health equipment, such as their Whoop devices, as they battle their squished fixture arrangements. Manager Tuchel has previously insisted it was the right thing for the squad to keep returning to Kansas as the players like to have a base. The squad like their “English-style hotel” and have a basketball area. Equally, although there were initial fears over the conditions here, England’s game will once again be played under a roof. The first game against Croatia in Dallas was also under a roof while the weather in Boston and New Jersey for the Ghana and Panama matches was considerably cooler than it was in England. And even the weather forecast for the weekend is 24C and rain. Tuchel, though, is now focusing on the clash with DR Congo with Djed Spence returning to the starting line-up in place of the injured Jarell Quansah. Tuchel’s only real decision is whether to start Marcus Rashford or Anthony Gordon on the left but the Manchester United star is set to keep his place.
