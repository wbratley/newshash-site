Ben Stokes retires from international cricket during third Test against New Zealand at Trent Bridge, aged 35

Unbiased summary

England Test captain Ben Stokes announced his retirement from international cricket on day four of the third Test against New Zealand at Trent Bridge on 28 June 2026. The 35-year-old all-rounder, who made his international debut in 2011, cited burnout and emotional exhaustion as primary factors, saying doubts had begun after England's 4-1 Ashes defeat in Australia and intensified during the Lord's Test. Stokes acknowledged that a separate incident involving a London nightclub, which led to him being made unavailable for the second Test at The Oval, 'added' to his decision, though he denied it was the direct cause. His international career spanned 122 Tests, 114 ODIs, and 43 T20s. Stokes took a wicket and batted in England's final chase before being dismissed for 30 in what proved his last international innings.

BBC News centre-left
Angle BBC News presents the retirement as Stokes' own considered, personally driven decision while carefully surfacing the unresolved questions around ECB support and the nightclub incident.
Bias The BBC provides the most factually complete account, including Stokes' direct quotes about burnout, the timeline of his doubts, and his acknowledgement that the nightclub controversy 'added' to his decision. Notably, Jonathan Agnew's accompanying analysis raises the unanswered question of whether Stokes felt supported by the ECB, and flags the unusual nature of announcing retirement mid-match with a series on the line. This is broadly balanced, though framing Stokes as 'one of the greatest cricketers to ever play for England' in the news report itself edges slightly into editorial territory.
