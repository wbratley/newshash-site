Sabalenka beats Osaka 7-5, 6-3 in first French Open women's night session match since 2023

Unbiased summary

World No 1 Aryna Sabalenka defeated Naomi Osaka 7-5, 6-3 in 87 minutes to advance to the quarter-finals of the French Open. The match was notable for being the first women's night session match at Roland Garros since 2023, a scheduling decision that has drawn attention given the relative rarity of women's matches being given the prime-time slot. The match generated significant interest and atmosphere, with both players drawing large audiences. The result itself was relatively straightforward, with Sabalenka as the dominant world No 1 progressing without major difficulty, while broader debate around gender equity in scheduling at the tournament accompanied the event.
