England and Wales record warmest spring since measurements began in 1884, Met Office provisional data confirms

Unbiased summary

Provisional Met Office data has confirmed that spring 2024 was the warmest on record for England and Wales since records began in 1884. England's average temperature for the three-month spring period reached 10.41°C, surpassing the previous record set last year by 0.18°C. The record was partly driven by an exceptionally early heatwave in May. The Met Office has also noted that some parts of the country face drought risk as a consequence of the dry, warm conditions. The data is described as provisional, meaning it may be subject to minor revision. The record extends a pattern of consecutive warm spring seasons in England and Wales.
