UK Home Office cancels travel authorisations for US commentators Hasan Piker and Cenk Uygur ahead of SXSW London appearances

Unbiased summary

The UK Home Office cancelled the Electronic Travel Authorisations (ETAs) of two American political commentators, Hasan Piker and Cenk Uygur, preventing them from entering Britain where they were scheduled to speak at SXSW London events. The Daily Mail reported the cancellations were linked to antisemitism concerns, while other outlets noted the decision drew criticism from political figures including Green Party deputy leader Zack Polanski, who called it 'really grim'. Free speech advocates also condemned the ban. Separately, Treasury minister Lord Spencer Livermore made headlines by stating it was his personal view that the UK would eventually rejoin the European Union, making him the first serving minister to publicly express such a position since Brexit.
