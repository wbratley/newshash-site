Southampton head coach Eckert initiated spying on rival clubs; owner Solak declines to sack him despite findings

Unbiased summary

Southampton head coach Tonda Eckert was found to have initiated a spying campaign against rival Championship clubs, during which interns were placed under pressure to record opponents' training sessions. WhatsApp messages leaked as part of an investigation revealed communications indicating Eckert's approval of the activity. Eckert has stated he was unaware his actions breached rules. Club owner Dragan Solak has decided not to dismiss Eckert, characterising the conduct as a 'mistake.' The club was thrown out of the Championship play-off final as a consequence of the spying. Additionally, non-league club Eastleigh may face separate related action. The investigation highlighted the vulnerability of junior staff, with at least one intern reportedly fearing job loss if they did not comply with requests to film opposition sessions.
