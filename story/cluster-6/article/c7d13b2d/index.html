The state - that bloated, disease bearing tick buried head first in the flesh of people like you and me. People who by now have long since had quite, quite enough. Well, okay, I might force myself, but in essence, all I really have now is go away. That's the limit of it. I have no more heightened blood pressure to offer, no more high dudgeon, no more righteous indignation of the sort that used to have me leap to my feet, or at least push to the edge of my seat. I say this because the state, the British state, together with its lick spittle, limp biscuit functionaries, is less than a joke. Utterly, utterly beneath the contempt of real people. Keir Starmer's announcement of a social media ban for under-16s, and therefore digital IDs as the necessary pass for everyone else wanting to do so much as use YouTube to learn how they might change the filter on a tumble dryer.
