Cape Verde qualify for World Cup last 32 on debut after 0-0 draw with Saudi Arabia, face Argentina next

Unbiased summary

Cape Verde drew 0-0 with Saudi Arabia in their final 2026 World Cup Group H match in Houston, securing qualification for the round of 32 on their tournament debut. The result, combined with Spain's 1-0 victory over Uruguay in Guadalajara, confirmed Cape Verde as group runners-up with three points from three draws. Saudi Arabia were eliminated. Cape Verde's players watched the closing moments of the Spain-Uruguay match on a mobile phone on the pitch before celebrating qualification. The nation of approximately 525,000-530,000 people becomes the smallest ever to reach the World Cup knockout stage. They will face defending champions Argentina in the last 32. Separately, Uruguay midfielder Manuel Ugarte was stretchered off with an apparent knee injury during the Spain match, and Saudi Arabia defender Hassan Al-Tambakti was also stretchered off during the Cape Verde game.
