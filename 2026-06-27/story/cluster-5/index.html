UK June temperature record broken for third consecutive day, reaching 37.3C in Suffolk amid heatwave and thunderstorms

Unbiased summary

The UK's record for the hottest June day was broken for a third consecutive day on Friday 27 June, with a provisional temperature of 37.3C recorded at Santon Downham, Suffolk, according to the Met Office. This surpassed Thursday's record of 36.7C set in Merryfield, Somerset, and Wednesday's 36.1C in Gosport, Hampshire. A red extreme heat warning was in place across London and south-east England, while amber warnings continued into Saturday. The heatwave caused NHS trusts to declare critical incidents, schools to close, and travel disruption across the country. Simultaneously, thunderstorms producing over 63,000 lightning strikes in 24 hours affected northern regions, causing fires and infrastructure incidents. Scientists attributed the event to human-driven climate change. Temperatures were expected to ease over the weekend.
