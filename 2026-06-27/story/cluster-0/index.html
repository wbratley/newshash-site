US strikes Iranian missile and drone sites after Iran attacks cargo ship in Strait of Hormuz, threatening fragile ceasefire

Unbiased summary

On Thursday, Iran's forces attacked the Singapore-flagged cargo ship M/V Ever Lovely with a drone as it transited the Strait of Hormuz. Iran said the vessel was using an unauthorised route; the US called it a ceasefire violation. The UN's International Maritime Organisation paused a planned evacuation of stranded vessels in response. The US military struck Iranian missile and drone storage facilities and coastal radar sites on Friday, describing it as a 'powerful response.' Iran's IRGC denied wrongdoing, blamed the US for violating the ceasefire, and claimed it struck US military positions in retaliation, threatening a more extensive response if attacks continued. Vice President Vance urged Iran to use diplomatic channels. The exchange represents the most serious test of the US-Iran ceasefire agreement.
