Spain beat Uruguay 1-0 to advance from World Cup Group H as Baena goal and Muslera error prove decisive

Unbiased summary

Spain defeated Uruguay 1-0 in their World Cup Group H final match in Guadalajara. The only goal came just before half-time when goalkeeper Fernando Muslera failed to stop Álex Baena's shot, with Muslera subsequently asking to be substituted at the interval. Uruguay's Manuel Ugarte suffered what appeared to be a serious knee injury before half-time, and Federico Valverde was substituted in the 56th minute. Late in the match, Uruguay's Agustín Canobbio received a straight red card for a foul on Pau Cubarsi, triggering a touchline confrontation between the two benches. Uruguay's elimination was confirmed as Cape Verde drew with Saudi Arabia, meaning Cape Verde finished second in the group. Uruguay manager Marcelo Bielsa accepted responsibility for failing to develop the squad's potential. England's progression to the knockout stage was confirmed as a consequence of Uruguay's exit.
