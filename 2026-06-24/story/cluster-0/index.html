England draw 0-0 with Ghana in World Cup Group L, remaining top ahead of final group game against Panama

Unbiased summary

England were held to a goalless draw by Ghana in their second 2026 World Cup group stage match at Boston Stadium. England dominated possession with approximately 78-79% of the ball but created few clear chances against a disciplined Ghana side coached by Carlos Queiroz, who deployed a deep 4-5-1 formation. England's best late opportunities included a Bukayo Saka shot saved by goalkeeper Benjamin Asare, a Nico O'Reilly header that struck the crossbar, and Harry Kane blazing over from close range from the rebound. Ezri Konsa escaped a potential penalty decision late on following a challenge on substitute Prince Kwabena Adu. Manager Thomas Tuchel made changes including starting Djed Spence and Marc Guehi, and was visibly animated on the touchline toward Spence during the second half. England remain top of Group L on four points, level with Ghana on points but ahead on goal difference, and face Panama in their final group game.
