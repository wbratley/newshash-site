Andy Burnham closes in on UK premiership as Labour leadership race shapes cabinet speculation and net zero policy debate intensifies

Unbiased summary

Following Keir Starmer's resignation as Prime Minister, Andy Burnham has emerged as the frontrunner to succeed him after winning the Makerfield by-election and facing no declared challengers. Burnham has been sworn in as an MP and held transition talks with Starmer. Attention has turned to potential cabinet appointments, with Ed Miliband, Wes Streeting, Shabana Mahmood and others discussed as possible chancellors. Miliband's net zero policies have drawn criticism from business figures, unions and US officials attending the ARC conference in London. Reports suggest Miliband may be prepared to soften his stance on North Sea gas drilling, potentially approving the Jackdaw gas field project, in order to strengthen his candidacy for chancellor. Angela Rayner has publicly advocated for social housing and welfare spending in what observers interpret as positioning for a cabinet role.
