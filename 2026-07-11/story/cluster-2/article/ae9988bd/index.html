Keira Knightley and James Righton silence marriage rumours with loved-up Wimbledon appearance The actress cheered from Centre Court as she and the musician enjoyed a rare public outing following months of speculation Don't Miss Most Read Keira Knightley and her husband James Righton made a united appearance at Wimbledon on Friday, walking hand in hand as they arrived at the All England Club. The couple, who have been married for 12 years, headed to Centre Court to support British tennis player Arthur Fery in his semi-final clash with German star Alexander Zverev. The 41-year-old actress appeared in high spirits throughout the afternoon, as she watched the gripping encounter unfold. At one point during the match, the Love Actually star leapt from her seat and cheered enthusiastically as the action intensified on court. Keira Knightley and James Righton attended the Wimbledon semi-finals at the All England Club |GETTY Their affectionate outing comes approximately three months after speculation about their marriage began swirling in February. The Black Doves star prompted concern when she altered her name on official business records, reverting from Keira Christina Righton to Keira Christina Knightley on documents for her performing arts company KCK Boo Ltd, which holds assets exceeding £11million. Adding to the rumours, the actress was photographed at a London event on February 23 without her diamond-encrusted wedding band, while her husband was also seen minus his ring. However, by April the pair appeared to have resolved any difficulties, with both spotted wearing their wedding rings once again. The actress wore a white Ralph Lauren dress for the Wimbledon outing |GETTY Representatives for the actress later clarified the name change was made to comply with new identity verification regulations, requiring alignment with her passport rather than her married name. For Friday's outing, Ms Knightley looked elegant in a white pussybow midi dress by Ralph Lauren, paired with strappy sandals, a white clutch bag and tortoiseshell sunglasses. Her husband, the former Klaxons frontman, opted for a light grey suit with a pink shirt and cream Gucci loafers. The couple, who share daughters Edie, ten, and Delilah, six, were seated alongside Richard E Grant and Lesley Manville.
